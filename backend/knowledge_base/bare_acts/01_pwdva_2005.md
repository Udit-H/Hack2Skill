# Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (PWDVA)

Act 43 of 2005. Enforced on 26 October 2006.
An Act to protect women from domestic violence occurring within the family.

## Chapter I — Preliminary

### Section 1: Short Title, Extent and Commencement
This Act may be called the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005. It extends to the whole of India.

### Section 2: Definitions
- **Aggrieved person**: Any woman who is or has been in a domestic relationship with the respondent and alleges domestic violence.
- **Child**: Any person below 18 years, includes adopted, step or foster child.
- **Domestic incident report (DIR)**: Report made in prescribed form (Form I) on receipt of complaint of domestic violence.
- **Domestic relationship**: Relationship between two persons who live or have lived together in a shared household, related by consanguinity, marriage, relationship in nature of marriage, adoption, or as joint family members.
- **Domestic violence**: Same meaning as in Section 3.
- **Magistrate**: Judicial Magistrate of First Class or Metropolitan Magistrate exercising jurisdiction where aggrieved person resides or respondent resides or domestic violence took place.
- **Monetary relief**: Compensation the Magistrate may order respondent to pay, including expenses and losses.
- **Protection Officer**: Officer appointed by State Government under Section 8.
- **Protection order**: Order made under Section 18.
- **Residence order**: Order granted under Section 19(1).
- **Respondent**: Any adult male person in domestic relationship with aggrieved person. An aggrieved wife may also file against relatives of husband.
- **Service provider**: Entity registered under Section 10.
- **Shared household**: Household where aggrieved person lives or has lived in domestic relationship, whether owned or tenanted by either or both parties, including joint family property.

## Chapter II — Definition of Domestic Violence

### Section 3: Definition of Domestic Violence
Any act, omission or conduct of the respondent constitutes domestic violence if it:
(a) Harms or endangers health, safety, life, limb or well-being (mental or physical) — includes physical abuse, sexual abuse, verbal and emotional abuse, economic abuse.
(b) Harasses to coerce unlawful dowry demand or property demand.
(c) Threatens the aggrieved person by conduct in (a) or (b).
(d) Otherwise injures or causes harm, physical or mental.

**Physical abuse**: Bodily pain, harm, danger to life/limb/health, assault, criminal intimidation, criminal force.
**Sexual abuse**: Any conduct of sexual nature that abuses, humiliates, degrades or violates dignity of woman.
**Verbal and emotional abuse**: Insults, ridicule, humiliation, name calling, insults regarding not having child/male child; repeated threats of physical pain.
**Economic abuse**: (a) Deprivation of economic/financial resources including household necessities, stridhan, property; (b) Disposal of household effects/assets without consent; (c) Restriction of access to resources/facilities/shared household.

## Chapter III — Powers and Duties of Protection Officers

### Section 4: Information to Protection Officer
Any person who has reason to believe domestic violence has been/is being/is likely to be committed may give information to the Protection Officer. No civil or criminal liability for giving information in good faith.

### Section 5: Duties of Police Officers, Service Providers and Magistrate
On receiving complaint of domestic violence, they shall inform the aggrieved person of:
(a) Right to apply for protection order, monetary relief, custody order, residence order, compensation order.
(b) Availability of service providers.
(c) Availability of Protection Officers.
(d) Right to free legal services under Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987.
(e) Right to file complaint under Section 498-A IPC (now BNS Section 85).

### Section 6: Duties of Shelter Homes
If aggrieved person requests shelter, the shelter home shall provide shelter.

### Section 7: Duties of Medical Facilities
If aggrieved person requests medical aid, the medical facility shall provide it.

### Section 8: Appointment of Protection Officers
State Government shall appoint Protection Officers in each district. Shall be women as far as possible.

### Section 9: Duties of Protection Officers
(a) Assist Magistrate. (b) Make domestic incident report (DIR) and forward to police and service providers. (c) Apply for protection order if aggrieved person desires. (d) Ensure legal aid under LSA Act. (e) Maintain list of service providers, shelter homes, medical facilities. (f) Arrange safe shelter. (g) Get medical examination. (h) Ensure monetary relief compliance. (i) Other prescribed duties.

### Section 10: Service Providers
Voluntary associations/companies registered with State Government can: record DIR, get medical examination, ensure shelter.

## Chapter IV — Procedure for Obtaining Orders

### Section 12: Application to Magistrate
(1) Aggrieved person, Protection Officer, or any other person on her behalf may apply to Magistrate for relief.
(2) Relief may include compensation/damages.
(3) Application in prescribed form.
(4) **First hearing: NOT ordinarily beyond 3 days from receipt of application.**
(5) **Disposal: Within 60 days from first hearing.**

### Section 13: Service of Notice
Notice served within maximum 2 days.

### Section 14: Counselling
Magistrate may direct counselling. Next hearing within 2 months.

### Section 17: Right to Reside in Shared Household
(1) Every woman in domestic relationship has right to reside in shared household, whether or not she has any right/title/interest.
(2) Cannot be evicted except by procedure established by law.

### Section 18: Protection Orders
Magistrate may pass protection order prohibiting respondent from:
(a) Committing domestic violence.
(b) Aiding/abetting domestic violence.
(c) Entering place of employment/school of aggrieved person.
(d) Attempting any communication — personal, oral, written, electronic, telephonic.
(e) Alienating assets, operating bank lockers/accounts without leave of Magistrate.
(f) Causing violence to dependants/relatives/persons assisting aggrieved person.
(g) Any other specified act.

### Section 19: Residence Orders
Magistrate may order:
(a) Restrain respondent from dispossessing aggrieved person from shared household.
(b) Direct respondent to remove himself from shared household.
(c) Restrain respondent/relatives from entering portion where aggrieved person resides.
(d) Restrain alienation of shared household.
(e) Restrain respondent from renouncing rights except with Magistrate's leave.
(f) **Direct respondent to secure alternate accommodation or pay rent.**
No order under (b) against a woman.

### Section 20: Monetary Relief
(1) Magistrate may order respondent to pay for: (a) Loss of earnings; (b) Medical expenses; (c) Loss from destruction/damage of property; (d) Maintenance for aggrieved person and children (in addition to S.125 CrPC maintenance).
(2) Must be adequate, fair, reasonable and consistent with standard of living.
(3) Can be lump sum or monthly payment.

### Section 21: Custody Orders
Magistrate may grant temporary custody of children to aggrieved person. May refuse respondent's visit if harmful to children.

### Section 22: Compensation Orders
Magistrate may order respondent to pay compensation for injuries including mental torture and emotional distress.

### Section 23: Interim and Ex Parte Orders
(1) Magistrate may pass interim orders as deemed just and proper.
(2) **May grant ex parte order based on affidavit if prima facie satisfied** that domestic violence has been/is being/is likely to be committed.

### Section 25: Duration of Orders
(1) Protection order remains in force till aggrieved person applies for discharge.
(2) Can be altered/modified/revoked on change of circumstances.

### Section 26: Relief in Other Proceedings
Relief under Sections 18-22 can also be sought in any civil, family, or criminal court proceedings.

### Section 27: Jurisdiction
Court of JMFC or Metropolitan Magistrate where:
(a) Aggrieved person resides (permanently or temporarily) or works.
(b) Respondent resides or works.
(c) Cause of action arose.
Any order enforceable throughout India.

### Section 29: Appeal
Appeal to Court of Session within 30 days.

## Chapter V — Miscellaneous

### Section 31: Penalty for Breach of Protection Order
(1) Breach of protection order is an offence: **imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to ₹20,000, or both.**
(2) Tried by same Magistrate who passed the order.
(3) **Offence is cognizable and non-bailable** (Section 32).
(4) Court may conclude offence committed on sole testimony of aggrieved person.

### Section 33: Penalty for Protection Officer's Failure
If Protection Officer fails duties: imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to ₹20,000, or both.

### Section 36: Not in Derogation
Provisions are in addition to, not in derogation of, other laws.

## Key Practical Points for Karnataka

### Filing in Bangalore
- File before Metropolitan Magistrate or JMFC having jurisdiction over your residence.
- No court fee for DV applications.
- Approach Protection Officer at District Women and Child Development Office.
- Can also approach any police station — they must record DIR and inform you of rights.

### Timeline
- First hearing: Within 3 days of application.
- Ex parte order: Can be obtained on same day if urgent.
- Disposal: Within 60 days.
- Appeal: Within 30 days to Sessions Court.

### Who Can File
- The woman herself.
- Protection Officer on her behalf.
- Any other person on her behalf (with her consent).
- Includes wives, live-in partners, mothers, sisters, widows, daughters living with abuser.
