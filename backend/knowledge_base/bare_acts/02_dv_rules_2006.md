# Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Rules, 2006

Notified by Central Government under PWDVA 2005. Contains prescribed forms and procedures.

## Rule 5: Domestic Incident Report (DIR) — Form I

### Who Records the DIR
- Protection Officer
- Service Provider (registered NGO)
- Police officer at any police station

### Contents of Form I (DIR)
The DIR must contain:
1. Details of aggrieved person (name, age, address, relationship with respondent).
2. Details of respondent (name, age, address, occupation).
3. Details of children (if any).
4. Nature of domestic violence suffered — physical, sexual, verbal/emotional, economic (tick applicable).
5. Description of incidents with dates.
6. Whether previously filed any complaint.
7. Relief sought — protection order, residence order, monetary relief, custody, compensation.
8. Details of injuries (if any).
9. Medical report (if obtained).
10. Signature of aggrieved person and Protection Officer/service provider.

### Important Notes on DIR
- DIR is NOT the same as FIR. DIR is a civil remedy document under PWDVA.
- Filing DIR does not automatically mean criminal case. It triggers civil protection proceedings.
- DIR can be filed BEFORE or AFTER approaching Magistrate.
- Police station MUST accept DIR and forward to Magistrate and Protection Officer.

## Rule 6: Applications to Magistrate — Form II

Form II is the application for seeking protection order and other reliefs. It includes:
1. Name of court and jurisdiction.
2. Particulars of aggrieved person and respondent.
3. Date of marriage/commencement of domestic relationship.
4. Facts relating to domestic violence.
5. Details of previous orders if any.
6. Relief(s) sought.
7. Affidavit.

## Rule 7: Affidavit — Form III

Form III is the affidavit to be filed along with application under Section 12.
Must be sworn before Magistrate or notary.

## Rule 8: Safety Plan

Protection Officer shall assist aggrieved person in making a safety plan for protection from further domestic violence. This includes:
- Identifying safe places to go in emergency.
- Important phone numbers (police, helpline, family, friends).
- Important documents to keep handy (ID, bank documents, medical records).
- Planning for children's safety.
- Emergency bag with essentials.

## Rule 15: Service Provider Registration

Service providers must register with District Magistrate/Collector. Registration valid for 3 years, renewable.

## Rule 17: Qualifications of Protection Officers

Protection Officers shall possess:
- Degree from recognized university.
- At least 3 years experience in social work/welfare.
- Women preferred.

## Forms Under DV Rules

### Form I — Domestic Incident Report (DIR)
Filed by Protection Officer or service provider. First step in DV proceedings.

### Form II — Application to Magistrate
Filed by aggrieved person or Protection Officer seeking protection order, residence order, monetary relief, custody, compensation.

### Form III — Affidavit
Filed along with application. Sworn statement of facts.

### Form IV — Protection Order
Issued by Magistrate under Section 18. Prohibits respondent from specified acts.

### Form V — Residence Order
Issued by Magistrate under Section 19. Protects aggrieved person's right to reside in shared household.

## DV Case Procedure Flowchart

### Step 1: Complaint/Report
- Approach Protection Officer, police station, service provider, or Magistrate directly.
- DIR (Form I) is recorded.

### Step 2: Application to Magistrate
- File Form II with affidavit (Form III).
- Can also be filed by Protection Officer on behalf of aggrieved person.

### Step 3: First Hearing
- Magistrate fixes first hearing within 3 days of receiving application.
- Notice served on respondent within 2 days.

### Step 4: Interim / Ex Parte Orders
- If urgent, Magistrate can pass ex parte order on first day based on affidavit alone.
- Interim protection order, residence order, monetary relief can be granted.

### Step 5: Hearing and Final Orders
- Both parties heard. Evidence recorded.
- Final protection order (Form IV), residence order (Form V), monetary relief, custody, compensation orders passed.
- Target disposal: 60 days from first hearing.

### Step 6: Enforcement
- Protection order enforceable throughout India.
- Breach is cognizable and non-bailable offence: up to 1 year imprisonment, ₹20,000 fine, or both.
- Police can arrest without warrant on breach.

### Step 7: Appeal
- Appeal to Sessions Court within 30 days.

## Timeline Summary for DV Cases in Karnataka

| Stage | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| DIR recording | Same day |
| Application to Magistrate | Same day or next day |
| First hearing | Within 3 days |
| Ex parte order | Same day as first hearing (if urgent) |
| Notice to respondent | Within 2 days of first hearing |
| Interim order | Within 1-2 weeks |
| Final disposal | Within 60 days |
| Appeal | Within 30 days of order |

## Maintenance Under DV Act (Section 20)

### Who Can Claim
- Aggrieved woman for herself.
- For her children.

### What It Covers
- Loss of earnings.
- Medical expenses.
- Loss from property damage.
- Monthly maintenance (food, clothing, shelter, medical, education of children).

### How Much
- Must be "adequate, fair and reasonable" and consistent with standard of living.
- No statutory cap (unlike S.125 CrPC which has limits in some states).
- Typically ranges from ₹5,000 to ₹50,000 per month depending on respondent's income and aggrieved person's needs.
- Interim maintenance can be ordered pending final disposal.

### In Addition To
- Maintenance under S.125 CrPC.
- Maintenance under Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act.
- Other maintenance orders.
