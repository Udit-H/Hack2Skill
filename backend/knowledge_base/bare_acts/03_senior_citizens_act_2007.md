# Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007

Act 56 of 2007. Enforced on 31 December 2007.
An Act to provide for more effective provisions for maintenance and welfare of parents and senior citizens guaranteed and recognized under the Constitution.

## Chapter I — Preliminary

### Section 2: Definitions
- **Children**: Son, daughter, grandson, granddaughter (NOT a minor).
- **Maintenance**: Provision for food, clothing, residence, medical attendance and treatment.
- **Parent**: Father or mother, whether biological or adoptive (step-parent included).
- **Relative**: Any legal heir other than children who possesses or would inherit property of senior citizen.
- **Senior citizen**: Any person who has attained the age of **60 years or above**.
- **Tribunal**: Maintenance Tribunal constituted under Section 7.
- **Welfare**: Provision of food, health care, recreation and any other amenity necessary for senior citizens.

## Chapter II — Maintenance of Parents and Senior Citizens

### Section 4: Maintenance of Parents and Senior Citizens
(1) A senior citizen including parent who is unable to maintain himself from his own earning or property, is entitled to make an application for maintenance.
(2) **Children and relatives are obligated to maintain parents/senior citizens.**
(3) If parent/senior citizen is childless, the relative who possesses property or will inherit property shall maintain.

### Section 5: Application for Maintenance
(1) Application may be made by:
- The senior citizen or parent himself/herself.
- Any person/organization authorized by senior citizen.
- The Tribunal may take suo motu cognizance on information received.
(2) Conciliation attempted first by the Tribunal.

### Section 6: Jurisdiction and Procedure
(1) Proceedings in area where parent/senior citizen resides.
(2) Evidence on affidavit.
(3) Party may be represented by advocate or authorized person.
(4) Proceedings are summary (simplified, not full trial).

### Section 7: Constitution of Maintenance Tribunal
(1) State Government constitutes Maintenance Tribunals at district level.
(2) Tribunal presided over by officer not below rank of Sub-Divisional Officer (SDO/SDM).

### Section 8: Summary Procedure
Tribunal follows summary procedure under CrPC.

### Section 9: Maximum Maintenance Amount
(1) **Maximum maintenance: ₹10,000 per month per parent/senior citizen.** (Note: This was the original amount. Many states including Karnataka have revised this upward. Current effective maximum is typically ₹10,000 but Tribunal has discretion.)
(2) Amount may be altered on proof of change in circumstances.

### Section 10: Maintenance Amount Deposit
Maintenance amount deposited within 30 days from order.

### Section 11: Penalty for Non-Payment
If children/relative fails to pay maintenance without sufficient cause: **imprisonment up to 1 month or until payment, whichever is earlier.**

## Chapter III — Establishment of Old Age Homes

### Section 19: Old Age Homes
(1) State Government shall establish at least one old age home in every district to accommodate at least 150 senior citizens.
(2) Must provide food, health care, and recreation.

### Section 20: Medical Support
State Government shall ensure provision of medical support, including bed in government hospital, for senior citizens.

## Chapter IV — Protection of Life and Property

### Section 22: Authorities for Implementation
State Government may specify authorities for dealing with senior citizen issues under this Act.

### Section 23: Transfer of Property — Void in Certain Circumstances
**(1) CRITICAL PROVISION**: If any senior citizen has transferred property (by gift or otherwise) subject to condition that transferee shall provide basic amenities and physical needs, and the transferee **refuses or fails** to provide such amenities:
- The said transfer shall be deemed to have been **made by fraud, coercion or undue influence**.
- The transfer shall be declared **VOID** by the Tribunal.

**(2)** Where senior citizen has right to receive maintenance out of estate, and such estate is transferred, the right to maintenance may be enforced against the transferee.

**Practical significance**: This is extremely powerful. If a son forces a father to transfer his house/property and then neglects him, the Tribunal can declare the entire transfer void and restore property to the senior citizen.

### Section 24: Exposure and Abandonment
Whoever having care/charge of a senior citizen leaves the senior citizen in any place with intention of wholly abandoning: **imprisonment up to 3 months or fine up to ₹5,000 or both.**

## Chapter V — Offences and Penalties

### Section 24 Details
- Offence of abandonment/exposure.
- Cognizable, non-bailable (if State Government notifies).
- Triable by Magistrate of First Class.

## Appeals

### Section 16: Appeals
(1) Any person aggrieved by order of Tribunal may appeal to Appellate Tribunal within **60 days**.
(2) Appellate Tribunal presided over by officer not below rank of District Magistrate.
(3) Appellate Tribunal may confirm, modify, annul or send back the order.
(4) **Maintenance shall continue to be paid during pendency of appeal.**

## Karnataka Implementation

### Karnataka Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Rules

In Karnataka, the Senior Citizens Act is implemented through:

**Maintenance Tribunals**: Constituted in every district. In Bangalore, the Tribunal operates at:
- Sub-Divisional Magistrate office, Bangalore Urban District.
- Revenue division offices in each sub-division.

**How to Apply in Karnataka**:
1. File application before the Maintenance Tribunal (SDM office) of the area where you reside.
2. No court fee required.
3. No lawyer required (but can have one).
4. Application can be in simple format describing your situation and relief sought.
5. Tribunal will issue notice to children/relatives.
6. First hearing typically within 15-30 days.
7. Tribunal attempts conciliation first.
8. If conciliation fails, evidence heard and order passed.
9. Maximum maintenance: ₹10,000 per month.
10. Order within 90 days of application.

**Contact for Karnataka**:
- District Social Welfare Officer (DSWO) in each district.
- Revenue Divisional Commissioner's office.

### Common Scenarios in Senior Citizen Cases

**Scenario 1: Children refusing maintenance**
- File application under Section 5.
- Tribunal orders monthly maintenance up to ₹10,000.
- Non-compliance leads to imprisonment up to 1 month.

**Scenario 2: Property transferred under coercion**
- File application under Section 23.
- If son took property promising care but now refuses, transfer declared void.
- Property reverts to senior citizen.
- This works even for registered sale deeds and gift deeds.

**Scenario 3: Abandoned by children**
- File complaint under Section 24.
- Criminal offence: imprisonment up to 3 months.
- Simultaneously file for maintenance.

**Scenario 4: Medical neglect**
- Falls under maintenance obligation (Section 2(b) — maintenance includes medical attendance and treatment).
- Can also approach District Social Welfare Officer.
- Government hospital beds reserved for senior citizens (Section 20).
