# Karnataka Rent Act, 1999 (Karnataka Act No. 34 of 2001)

Also known as Karnataka Rent Control Act. Replaced the old Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961 for new tenancies.
Governs landlord-tenant relationships in Karnataka.

## Key Definitions

### Landlord
Any person entitled to receive rent for premises let out, including legal heirs and assignees.

### Tenant
Person to whom premises are let and includes sub-tenant and any person continuing in possession after termination of tenancy. Does NOT include trespasser.

### Premises
Any building or part of building let for residential or commercial use. Includes land appurtenant.

### Fair Rent
Rent determined by Rent Controller considering location, amenities, and prevailing market conditions.

## Grounds for Eviction (Section 21)

A landlord CANNOT evict a tenant except on the following grounds (filed before Rent Controller):

### Ground 1: Non-Payment of Rent
Tenant has not paid rent for 2 consecutive months after written demand. But tenant can deposit arrears and court costs to avoid eviction (relief against forfeiture).

### Ground 2: Subletting Without Consent
Tenant has sublet the premises without written consent of landlord.

### Ground 3: Nuisance or Damage
Tenant has committed nuisance, caused damage, or used premises for immoral/illegal purposes.

### Ground 4: Bona Fide Personal Need
Landlord requires premises for bona fide personal occupation or for family members. Landlord must not have other reasonably suitable accommodation.

### Ground 5: Reconstruction/Repair
Premises require substantial repair/reconstruction which cannot be carried out without vacating.

### Ground 6: Change of Use
Tenant has changed the agreed use of premises without landlord's consent (e.g., residential to commercial).

## Notice Requirements

### For Termination of Tenancy
- **Monthly tenancy**: Minimum **15 days' notice** before end of month of tenancy.
- **Yearly tenancy**: Minimum **6 months' notice**.
- Notice must be in writing.
- Notice must state the ground for eviction.
- **Oral notice is NOT valid.**

### Notice for Non-Payment of Rent
- Written demand for arrears.
- Tenant gets opportunity to pay within time granted by Rent Controller.
- If rent paid within court-granted time, eviction petition dismissed.

## Section 27: Tenant's Right to Occupy

Even after expiry of lease period or termination of tenancy by notice, tenant continues to be a **deemed tenant** and CANNOT be evicted except through order of Rent Controller on grounds specified in Section 21.

**Key principle**: A landlord cannot take law into own hands. Even if lease has expired, tenant cannot be forcibly evicted. Only the Rent Controller can order eviction.

## Illegal Actions by Landlord (What Is NOT Allowed)

### 1. Forcible Eviction
Landlord cannot physically throw tenant out, change locks, or remove tenant's belongings. This is criminal offence under BNS (wrongful restraint S.126, criminal intimidation S.329, criminal trespass).

### 2. Utility Disconnection
Cutting water supply, electricity, or gas to force tenant to leave is ILLEGAL in Karnataka.
- This constitutes criminal intimidation under BNS Section 329.
- Tenant can file FIR and also get injunction from Rent Controller.
- Can also complain to BESCOM (electricity) and BWSSB (water) who may take action against illegal disconnection.

### 3. Harassment / Threats
Threatening tenant, verbal abuse, sending goons — all criminal offences.
- BNS Section 329: Criminal intimidation — up to 2 years imprisonment.
- BNS Section 351: Assault.
- BNS Section 126: Wrongful restraint.

### 4. Unauthorized Entry
Landlord entering tenant's premises without permission is criminal trespass under BNS Section 329.

## Security Deposit Rules

### Maximum Deposit
- Under Karnataka Rent Act: No specific cap mentioned, but typically 10 months' rent is standard practice.
- Under Model Tenancy Act 2021 (if adopted): Maximum 2 months' rent for residential.
- Common practice in Bangalore: 6-10 months' rent.

### Refund of Security Deposit
- Must be refunded when tenant vacates, subject to deductions for damage (not normal wear and tear).
- If landlord refuses to refund, tenant can:
  1. Send legal notice demanding refund within 30 days.
  2. File suit in Small Causes Court for recovery.
  3. File complaint with Rent Controller.
- Landlord CANNOT forfeit deposit without valid reason.
- Normal wear and tear is NOT grounds for deduction.

## Rent Increase Rules

### When Can Rent Be Increased
- As per agreement between parties.
- If no agreement, landlord can apply to Rent Controller for revision.
- Standard increase: 5-10% per year is typical practice in Bangalore.
- Increase cannot be arbitrary or unreasonable.

### Unfair Rent Increase
If tenant believes rent increase is unfair:
- Can apply to Rent Controller for determination of fair rent.
- Rent Controller considers: location, amenities, condition, market rates, age of building.

## How to File Eviction Case (Landlord's Process)

1. Send written notice to tenant with ground for eviction and notice period.
2. If tenant does not vacate, file petition before Rent Controller.
3. Rent Controller is the Court of Small Causes Judge (in Bangalore) or Civil Judge (in other areas).
4. Case heard, evidence recorded.
5. If grounds proved, Rent Controller orders eviction with reasonable time to vacate.
6. If tenant still doesn't vacate, execution proceedings.

## How to Defend Against Eviction (Tenant's Process)

1. Verify if proper written notice was given with correct notice period.
2. Verify if grounds are valid under Section 21.
3. If non-payment: deposit arrears + costs with court to get relief.
4. If landlord claims personal need: challenge genuineness — ask for evidence of need.
5. File written statement denying grounds.
6. If illegal eviction attempted: (a) File FIR; (b) Apply for temporary injunction; (c) Approach Rent Controller immediately.

## Jurisdiction in Bangalore

### Where to File
- Rent Controller: Court of Small Causes, Bangalore (for areas within BBMP limits).
- Location: Near City Civil Court complex, Nrupathunga Road.
- For areas outside BBMP: Civil Judge of the area.

### Court Fees
- Eviction petition: ₹200-500 (ad valorem based on annual rent).
- Injunction application: Additional ₹100-200.
- Stamp paper for affidavit: ₹20 non-judicial stamp paper.
