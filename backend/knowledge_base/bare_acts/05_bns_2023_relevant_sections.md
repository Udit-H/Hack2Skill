# Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS) — Relevant Sections

BNS 2023 replaced the Indian Penal Code, 1860 with effect from 1 July 2024.
Below are sections relevant to domestic violence, eviction, and senior citizen abuse cases.

## Domestic Violence Related Offences

### Section 85: Cruelty by Husband or Relatives (Replaces IPC 498A)
Whoever, being the husband or the relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such woman to cruelty shall be punished with **imprisonment up to 3 years and fine**.

**Cruelty means**:
(a) Any wilful conduct likely to drive the woman to commit suicide or cause grave injury to life, limb, or health (mental or physical).
(b) Harassment with a view to coercing her or any related person to meet unlawful demand for property or valuable security.

**Key points**:
- Cognizable offence (police can arrest without warrant).
- Non-bailable.
- Can be compounded with permission of Court.
- Filed at police station where woman resides or where offence committed.
- Investigation by officer not below rank of Inspector.

### Section 86: Dowry Death (Replaces IPC 304B)
Where death of a woman is caused by burns, bodily injury, or occurs otherwise than under normal circumstances **within 7 years of marriage**, and she was subjected to cruelty or harassment for dowry: **imprisonment for minimum 7 years, extendable to imprisonment for life.**

Presumption of dowry death applies if woman subjected to cruelty before death.

## Physical Violence Offences

### Section 115: Voluntarily Causing Hurt (Replaces IPC 323)
Whoever voluntarily causes hurt: **imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to ₹10,000, or both.**
Hurt means causing bodily pain, disease, or infirmity.

Applies to: DV physical violence, eviction-related assault, elder abuse.

### Section 117: Voluntarily Causing Grievous Hurt (Replaces IPC 325)
Whoever voluntarily causes grievous hurt: **imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.**
Grievous hurt includes: emasculation, permanent privation of sight/hearing, fracture/dislocation of bone, destruction of member or joint, any hurt endangering life.

### Section 118: Causing Hurt by Dangerous Weapons (Replaces IPC 324)
Using dangerous weapon, heated substance, poison, corrosive substance, explosive, fire or animal: **imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.**

## Restraint and Intimidation Offences

### Section 126: Wrongful Restraint (Replaces IPC 341)
Whoever voluntarily obstructs any person so as to prevent that person from proceeding in any direction: **simple imprisonment up to 1 month, or fine up to ₹5,000, or both.**

Applies to: Landlord blocking tenant from entering premises, husband preventing wife from leaving house.

### Section 127: Wrongful Confinement (Replaces IPC 342)
Whoever wrongfully confines any person: **imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to ₹5,000, or both.**
Keeping someone locked in a room, house, etc.

### Section 329: Criminal Intimidation (Replaces IPC 506)
Whoever threatens another with injury to person, reputation, or property, or threatens to impute unchastity to a woman, with intent to cause alarm or compel the person to do/omit something:
- **Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.**
- If threat is to cause death, grievous hurt, or fire to property: **imprisonment up to 7 years, or fine, or both.**

Applies to: Landlord threatening tenant, husband threatening wife, children threatening elderly parent.

## Assault Offences

### Section 351: Criminal Force (Replaces IPC 350/352)
Whoever intentionally uses force to any person without their consent, intending to cause injury, fear, or annoyance: **imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to ₹1,000, or both.**

### Section 352: Assault (Replaces IPC 351/355)
Whoever makes any gesture or preparation intending to cause apprehension that criminal force is about to be used: same punishment as criminal force.

## Property and Trespass Offences

### Section 329: Criminal Trespass (Replaces IPC 441/447)
Whoever enters/remains in property in possession of another with intent to commit offence, intimidate, insult, or annoy: **imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to ₹5,000, or both.**

**House-trespass** (entering a building used as human dwelling): **imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to ₹5,000, or both.**

Applies to: Landlord forcibly entering tenant's premises, breaking locks.

### Section 331: Lurking House-Trespass or House-Breaking
Entering building at night, by force, etc: enhanced punishment up to **3-10 years depending on circumstances.**

## Relevance to Our Crisis Tracks

### For Eviction Cases
- Landlord forcible entry: Criminal trespass, house-trespass
- Landlord threats: Criminal intimidation (S.329) — up to 2 years
- Landlord cutting utilities: Criminal intimidation, wrongful restraint
- Physical assault by landlord/goons: S.115 (hurt), S.351/352 (assault/force)

### For DV Cases
- Physical violence: S.115 (hurt), S.117 (grievous hurt), S.118 (dangerous weapons)
- Threats: S.329 (criminal intimidation)
- Cruelty: S.85 (cruelty by husband/relatives)
- Dowry-related death: S.86 (dowry death)
- Confinement: S.127 (wrongful confinement)

### For Senior Citizen Cases
- Physical abuse: S.115 (hurt), S.117 (grievous hurt)
- Threats/intimidation: S.329 (criminal intimidation)
- Forcible eviction from own home: S.126 (wrongful restraint), criminal trespass
- Abandonment: Senior Citizens Act S.24 + BNS provisions

## IPC to BNS Transition Note

BNS 2023 came into effect on 1 July 2024, replacing the Indian Penal Code 1860. During the transition period (2024-2025), some courts and police stations may still reference IPC section numbers. Here is the mapping for relevant sections:

| Old IPC Section | New BNS Section | Offence |
|----------------|----------------|---------|
| 323 | 115 | Voluntarily causing hurt |
| 324 | 118 | Hurt with dangerous weapons |
| 325 | 117 | Voluntarily causing grievous hurt |
| 341 | 126 | Wrongful restraint |
| 342 | 127 | Wrongful confinement |
| 350/352 | 351 | Criminal force |
| 351/355 | 352 | Assault |
| 441/447 | 329 | Criminal trespass |
| 498A | 85 | Cruelty by husband/relatives |
| 304B | 86 | Dowry death |
| 506 | 329 | Criminal intimidation |

Note: FIRs filed under old IPC sections before 1 July 2024 continue under IPC. New cases after 1 July 2024 must be under BNS.
