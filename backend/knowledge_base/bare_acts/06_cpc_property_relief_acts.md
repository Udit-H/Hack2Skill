# Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 — Order XXXIX (Temporary Injunctions) and Order XXI (Execution)

## Order XXXIX: Temporary Injunctions and Interlocutory Orders

### Rule 1: Cases in Which Temporary Injunction May Be Granted
The Court may grant temporary injunction where:
(a) Property in dispute is in danger of being wasted, damaged, or alienated by any party.
(b) Defendant threatens or intends to remove or dispose of property to defraud creditors.
(c) Defendant threatens to dispossess plaintiff or otherwise cause injury to plaintiff in relation to property in dispute.

**Application to eviction**: If landlord is threatening illegal eviction, tenant can apply for temporary injunction under this Rule to prevent dispossession pending trial.

### Rule 2: Injunction to Restrain Repetition or Continuance of Breach
Court may grant temporary injunction to restrain breach of:
- Any contract or agreement.
- Any obligation arising from law.

**Application**: If landlord is continuously harassing tenant, cutting utilities, or threatening eviction, court can restrain continued breach.

### Rule 3: Notice to Opposite Party Before Granting Injunction
(a) Court shall not grant injunction without giving notice to opposite party and hearing them.
(b) **Exception (Ex Parte Injunction)**: If delay will cause irreparable loss or property will be destroyed or removed, Court may grant **ex parte injunction** (without hearing other side).
(c) Ex parte injunction shall be limited in duration — Court must fix date for hearing opposite party.

### Rule 4: Order for Injunction May Be Discharged, Varied or Set Aside
Injunction may be modified or set aside on application by either party.

### Three Tests for Injunction (From Supreme Court Precedent)
Court considers three factors before granting temporary injunction:
1. **Prima facie case**: Plaintiff must show a reasonable basis for their claim.
2. **Balance of convenience**: Which party suffers more if injunction is/is not granted.
3. **Irreparable injury**: Injury that cannot be adequately compensated by damages.

### Procedure for Filing Injunction in Karnataka

1. **Draft application**: Petition under Order XXXIX Rule 1/2 CPC with supporting affidavit.
2. **File with court**: File with Civil Judge having jurisdiction. In Bangalore, City Civil Court or appropriate Munsiff Court.
3. **Court fee**: ₹100-500 depending on subject matter value (Karnataka Court Fees Schedule).
4. **Ex parte hearing**: If urgent, court may hear application same day and grant ex parte injunction.
5. **Notice to respondent**: If ex parte order granted, respondent gets notice. Hearing fixed within 30 days.
6. **Final hearing**: Both sides heard, temporary injunction confirmed/modified/vacated.
7. **Duration**: Temporary injunction remains until final disposal of suit or further order.

## Order XXI: Execution of Decrees and Orders

### When Does This Apply
After you WIN a case — when court has passed a decree or order and the losing party does not comply.

### Rule 32: Decree for Specific Performance or Restitution of Property
Court may order specific performance of contract or delivery of immovable property.
- If someone is ordered to hand over property and refuses, court can direct forcible delivery through executing officer.

### Rule 35: Decree for Delivery of Immovable Property
(1) Where decree is for delivery of immovable property, executing court shall order delivery by removing any person refusing to vacate.
(2) Court may use police force if necessary.

### Execution Process
1. **File Execution Application**: After getting favorable decree, file application for execution in same court or court having jurisdiction over property/person.
2. **Notice to Judgment Debtor**: Court issues notice giving time to comply.
3. **If Non-Compliance**: Court may order:
   - Attachment and sale of property.
   - Arrest and detention (civil prison).
   - Appointment of receiver.
   - Delivery of property through court officer with police assistance.

### Application to Our Cases

**Eviction — After Getting Injunction**:
If landlord violates court's injunction order (e.g., still cuts utilities despite court order), tenant can:
1. File contempt petition under Contempt of Courts Act.
2. File execution application for enforcement of injunction.

**DV — Protection Order Enforcement**:
Protection orders under PWDVA are enforced through Magistrate directly (S.31 — breach is cognizable). But if civil court orders are violated, execution under Order XXI.

**Senior Citizens — Maintenance Order Enforcement**:
If children don't pay maintenance ordered by Tribunal:
- Tribunal can imprison for up to 1 month under Senior Citizens Act S.11.
- For civil court maintenance orders, execution under Order XXI (attachment of salary/bank accounts).

## Specific Relief Act, 1963 — Section 6: Suit by Dispossessed Person

### Section 6: Suit by Person Dispossessed of Immovable Property
If any person is dispossessed of immovable property otherwise than by due course of law:
- That person or any person claiming through them may bring a suit **within 6 months** from date of dispossession.
- The court shall restore possession if satisfied that dispossession was without authority of law.
- **No question of title shall be considered** in such suit.

**Application**: If a landlord illegally throws out a tenant, tenant can file suit under Section 6 within 6 months and get possession restored WITHOUT having to prove ownership. Only need to prove you were in possession and were unlawfully dispossessed.

### Key Points
- Time limit: **6 months from dispossession** (very strict).
- No title question: Court only looks at possession and whether dispossession was lawful.
- Quick remedy: Summary procedure possible.
- Works for tenants, licensees, even those without formal lease agreement.

## Transfer of Property Act, 1882

### Section 106: Duration of Certain Leases
(1) If lease is for agricultural or manufacturing purposes: deemed to be lease from year to year, terminable by **6 months' notice**.
(2) If lease for other purposes (residential, commercial): deemed to be lease from month to month, terminable by **15 days' notice** expiring with end of a month of tenancy.
- Notice must be in writing.
- Notice period runs from end of month in which notice is received.

### Section 111: Determination of Lease
Lease can end by:
(a) Efflux of time stated in lease.
(b) Happening of specified event.
(c) Merger of interests.
(d) Express surrender by tenant to landlord.
(e) Implied surrender.
(f) Forfeiture (tenant breaches condition).
(g) Notice to quit (as per S.106).

Even after determination under S.111, in Karnataka, tenant continues as deemed tenant under Karnataka Rent Act and cannot be evicted without Rent Controller's order.
