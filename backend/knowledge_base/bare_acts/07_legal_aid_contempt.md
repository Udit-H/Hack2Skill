# Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 — Free Legal Aid

## Purpose
Provides for free legal services to weaker sections of society and organizes Lok Adalats for amicable settlement of disputes.

## Section 12: Criteria for Giving Legal Services

Every person who has to file or defend a case shall be entitled to legal services if they belong to ANY of the following categories:

### (a) Member of Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe

### (b) Victim of Trafficking or Begar
Person rescued from trafficking in human beings or begar.

### (c) Woman or Child
**Any woman or child** is entitled to free legal aid regardless of income.

### (d) Person with Disability
Person with disability as defined in the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016.

### (e) Victim of Mass Disaster, Violence, Flood, Drought, Earthquake, Industrial Disaster
Victims of mass disaster, ethnic violence, caste atrocity, flood, drought, earthquake, or industrial disaster.

### (f) Industrial Workman
Worker under the Industrial Disputes Act.

### (g) In Custody
Person in custody including under-trial prisoner.

### (h) Person with Annual Income Below Prescribed Amount
Person whose annual income does not exceed the prescribed amount.

**Karnataka Income Ceiling for Free Legal Aid: ₹3,00,000 per annum (₹3 lakhs/year).**

For Supreme Court matters: ₹5,00,000 per annum.

### (i) Victim of Domestic Violence
Under PWDVA 2005, every aggrieved woman is entitled to free legal aid.

## What Free Legal Aid Includes

1. **Free lawyer**: Advocate appointed by Legal Services Authority to represent you.
2. **Court fees waiver**: All court fees and process fees exempted.
3. **Document preparation**: Free drafting of applications, petitions, affidavits.
4. **Certified copies**: Free certified copies of judgments and orders.
5. **Legal advice**: Free consultation on legal matters.

## How to Apply for Free Legal Aid in Karnataka

### Step 1: Apply to KSLSA
Apply at:
- **Karnataka State Legal Services Authority (KSLSA)**: Nyaya Seva Soudha, M.G. Road area, Bangalore.
- **District Legal Services Authority (DLSA)**: At each district court complex.
- **Taluk Legal Services Committee**: At taluk court complex.
- **Legal Aid Clinics**: In many police stations, jails, and community centers.

### Step 2: Submit Application
- Simple application form (available at DLSA counter).
- Attach identity proof.
- Attach income proof (if claiming under income category):
  - Ration card
  - Income certificate from Tahsildar
  - Self-declaration of income
- For women, SC/ST, disabled: No income proof needed.

### Step 3: Verification and Appointment
- DLSA verifies eligibility (usually within 1-2 days).
- Panel advocate assigned.
- Advocate represents you in court at no cost.

## NALSA (National Legal Services Authority) Regulations

### NALSA Regulations on Free Legal Aid, 2010
- Legal Services Authority cannot reject an application merely on technical grounds.
- If applicant is prima facie eligible, legal aid must be provided first and verified later.
- Quality of legal aid must be monitored. If panel advocate is not performing, another advocate shall be assigned.

### Helpline
- **NALSA Legal Aid Helpline: 15100** (toll-free, 24/7)
- **Tele-Law Service**: Free legal advice via phone/video call by panel lawyers.

## Key Points for Users

1. **You do NOT need to hire a private lawyer** if you qualify for free legal aid.
2. **Women always qualify** regardless of income.
3. **SC/ST/disabled always qualify** regardless of income.
4. **For everyone else**: Income below ₹3 lakhs/year qualifies.
5. **No cost at all**: No court fees, no lawyer fees, no copying charges.
6. **Quality assurance**: If your lawyer is not responsive, complain to DLSA for replacement.
7. **Available for all courts**: Civil, criminal, family, consumer, tribunal.

## Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 — Enforcement of Court Orders

### Section 2(b): Civil Contempt
Wilful disobedience of any judgment, decree, direction, order, writ, or other process of a court. Also includes wilful breach of an undertaking given to the court.

### When to Use
- Landlord violates injunction order (e.g., continues cutting utilities despite court order).
- DV respondent violates protection order (also punishable under PWDVA S.31, but contempt is additional remedy).
- Children violate maintenance order from Senior Citizens Tribunal.

### Punishment
- Imprisonment up to 6 months, or fine up to ₹2,000, or both.
- Continuing contempt: Additional punishment until compliance.

### Procedure
1. File contempt petition in the court that passed the original order.
2. Court issues notice to contemnor (person in contempt).
3. Hearing. If contempt proved, punishment ordered.
4. Contemnor may purge contempt by complying with order + apology.

### Section 12: Punishment for Contempt
- Court may punish on its own motion or on petition by aggrieved party.
- Maximum: 6 months imprisonment and ₹2,000 fine.
