# Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (BSA) — Evidence Law

BSA 2023 replaced the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 with effect from 1 July 2024.
Governs what is admissible as evidence in Indian courts.

## What Counts as Evidence

### Section 2: Definitions
- **Evidence**: All statements which the court permits or requires to be made before it (oral evidence), and all documents and electronic records produced for inspection (documentary evidence).
- **Document**: Any matter expressed or described upon any substance by means of letters, figures, or marks.
- **Electronic record**: Data, record, or data generated, image or sound stored, received, or sent in electronic form.

### Section 57: Oral Evidence (Replaces IEA S.59-60)
All facts except contents of documents and electronic records may be proved by oral evidence.
Oral evidence must be direct — the person who saw/heard/perceived the fact must testify.

### Section 58: Proof of Contents of Documents
Contents of documents and electronic records may be proved by primary evidence (original) or secondary evidence (copies).

## Electronic Evidence — Critical for Modern Cases

### Section 63: Admissibility of Electronic Records (Replaces IEA S.65B)
Electronic records are admissible as evidence if:
1. Produced from a computer/device that was regularly used to store/process information.
2. Information was regularly fed into the computer in the ordinary course of activities.
3. The computer was operating properly.
4. The information reproduces or is derived from information fed into the computer.

**Certificate requirement**: A certificate must accompany electronic evidence identifying the electronic record, describing the manner of its production, and certifying that the conditions above are satisfied. Certificate signed by responsible person.

### What Electronic Evidence Is Admissible

**WhatsApp Messages**: YES, admissible if:
- Screenshots taken properly (showing phone number, date, time).
- Certificate under Section 63 provided (signed by person who took screenshots).
- Phone preserved for verification if needed.

**SMS/Text Messages**: YES, same conditions as WhatsApp.

**Emails**: YES, with printout and certificate.

**CCTV Footage**: YES, with certificate from person in charge of CCTV system.

**Call Recordings**: YES, if one party to the call recorded it. Recording by third party without knowledge of parties is generally not admissible.

**Social Media Posts (Facebook, Instagram)**: YES, screenshots with URL, date, time, and certificate.

**Photos**: YES, with metadata if available. Certificate confirming authenticity.

**Videos**: YES, with certificate. Unedited footage preferred.

## How to Submit Electronic Evidence Properly

### Step 1: Preserve Original
Keep the original device (phone, laptop) with the evidence. Do not delete messages/files.

### Step 2: Take Screenshots/Printouts
- Take clear screenshots showing complete context (date, time, sender, content).
- For WhatsApp: Show contact name, phone number, full conversation.
- For photos: Show metadata (date, time, location if available).

### Step 3: Prepare Section 63 Certificate
A certificate stating:
- I, [Name], [Designation], hereby certify that:
- The electronic record described herein is produced from [device description].
- The device was regularly used for [purpose].
- The device was operating properly.
- The information is accurately reproduced.
- Signed, dated.

### Step 4: Notarize If Possible
Getting the certificate notarized strengthens evidentiary value.

### Step 5: Present to Court
File the certificate along with printouts/copies as evidence. Keep original device available for inspection.

## Photo and Video Evidence Guidelines

### For Injuries (DV Cases)
- Photograph injuries as soon as possible after incident.
- Include close-up and wider shots showing context.
- If possible, include a newspaper/dated object showing date.
- Get MLC (Medico-Legal Certificate) from hospital.
- Photos supplement but do NOT replace medical evidence.

### For Property Damage (Eviction Cases)
- Photograph the damage — broken locks, water/electricity meters, damaged property.
- Include wide shots of premises.
- Take video walkthrough if possible.
- Get witnesses if possible.

### For Harassment/Threats (All Cases)
- Record threatening calls (one-party consent is generally allowed in India).
- Save threatening messages (WhatsApp, SMS, email).
- Take screenshots with timestamps.
- If threats are verbal in person, note time/date/place/witnesses immediately.

## Expert Evidence

### Section 45: Opinion of Expert (BSA Section 39)
When court needs opinion on: foreign law, science, art, identity of handwriting, identity of finger impressions — opinion of persons specially skilled (experts) is relevant.

### Medical Evidence
- MLC (Medico-Legal Certificate) from doctor is expert evidence.
- Doctor who examined the patient can be called as expert witness.
- Medical reports from government hospitals carry significant weight.

## Presumptions About Documents

### Section 79: Presumption as to Genuineness of Certified Copies
Court presumes that certified copies of government documents are genuine.

### Section 85: Presumption as to Electronic Records
Court may presume that electronic records are genuine if properly certified under Section 63.

## Practical Checklist for Evidence Collection

### For DV Cases
- [ ] Photographs of injuries (with date reference)
- [ ] MLC from hospital (immediately after incident)
- [ ] Screenshots of threatening messages
- [ ] Record of threatening calls
- [ ] Witnesses (neighbors, relatives)
- [ ] DIR (Domestic Incident Report) — file at police station
- [ ] Any previous complaints/FIRs
- [ ] Medical records of ongoing treatment
- [ ] Proof of economic abuse (bank statements showing control)

### For Eviction Cases
- [ ] Copy of rent agreement/lease deed
- [ ] Rent receipts/bank transfer records
- [ ] Photographs of any damage/harassment
- [ ] Written notice from landlord (if any)
- [ ] Screenshots of threatening messages
- [ ] Witnesses (neighbors)
- [ ] BESCOM/BWSSB bills showing disconnection
- [ ] Video of landlord's agents attempting eviction

### For Senior Citizen Cases
- [ ] Property documents (if transfer involved)
- [ ] Medical records showing neglect
- [ ] Photographs/video of living conditions
- [ ] Bank statements (if financial abuse)
- [ ] Witness statements (neighbors, friends)
- [ ] Previous requests for maintenance (written)
