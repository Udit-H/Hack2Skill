# Government Schemes — Karnataka

> Key government welfare schemes relevant to crisis situations: housing, healthcare, women's safety, and financial support.

---

## 1. Housing Schemes

### PM Awas Yojana — Gramin (PMAY-G)
- **What**: Pucca house with basic amenities
- **Benefit**: ₹1,20,000 (plain area) / ₹1,30,000 (hilly area)
- **Eligibility**: Houseless families, families in kutcha/dilapidated houses (identified via SECC data)
- **How to apply**: Through Gram Panchayat → Block Development Officer
- **Website**: pmayg.nic.in
- **Relevance**: Eviction victims who are homeless and from rural areas

### PM Awas Yojana — Urban (PMAY-U)
- **What**: Affordable housing in urban areas
- **Benefit**: Interest subsidy (6.5% for EWS/LIG), in-situ slum redevelopment
- **Eligibility**: Annual income up to ₹3 lakh (EWS), ₹6 lakh (LIG)
- **How to apply**: Through Urban Local Body (BBMP/Municipality) or Common Service Centre
- **Website**: pmaymis.gov.in
- **Relevance**: Urban eviction victims needing permanent housing

### Basava Vasati Yojana (Karnataka State)
- **What**: Housing assistance for BPL families
- **Benefit**: ₹1,20,000 for house construction
- **Eligibility**: Karnataka BPL families, priority for SC/ST, women-headed households
- **How to apply**: Rajiv Gandhi Housing Corporation (RGRHCL) office

### Ashraya Housing Scheme (Karnataka)
- **What**: Houses for shelterless BPL families
- **Benefit**: House allotment / construction assistance
- **Eligibility**: Houseless BPL families identified by Gram Panchayat
- **How to apply**: Through Zilla Panchayat

## 2. Women's Safety & Empowerment Schemes

### One-Stop Centre (Sakhi) — Sambal Scheme
- **What**: Integrated support for women affected by violence
- **Services**: Emergency response, medical aid, legal aid, psycho-social counselling, shelter, police facilitation
- **Eligibility**: ALL women affected by violence (no income/caste restriction)
- **How to access**: Call **181** (Women Helpline) or visit directly
- **Locations**: Available in all 31 districts of Karnataka
- **Relevance**: DV victims, eviction victims — first point of contact

### Ujjwala Scheme (Anti-Trafficking)
- **What**: Rescue, rehabilitation, and reintegration of trafficking victims
- **Services**: Shelter homes, legal aid, counselling, vocational training
- **Eligibility**: Women and children who are victims of trafficking
- **How to access**: Through NGOs or Women & Child Development Department

### Swadhar Greh
- **What**: Shelter for women in difficult circumstances
- **Services**: Temporary shelter (up to 3 years), food, clothing, medical care, legal aid, vocational training
- **Eligibility**: Destitute women, women in distress, DV survivors, released female prisoners, trafficked women
- **How to access**: Through District Women & Child Development Office
- **Relevance**: DV survivors needing long-term shelter + rehabilitation

### Mahila Shakti Kendra
- **What**: Community-level support for rural women
- **Services**: Awareness, training, digital literacy, convergence of schemes
- **Eligibility**: All rural women
- **How to access**: Through Block/District administration

### STREE Scheme (Karnataka)
- **What**: Support to Training and Employment for women
- **Benefit**: Skill development + placement assistance
- **Eligibility**: Women from economically weaker sections
- **How to apply**: Through Karnataka Skill Development Corporation

## 3. Healthcare Schemes

### Ayushman Bharat — PM-JAY
- **What**: Health insurance for poor and vulnerable families
- **Benefit**: ₹5,00,000 per family per year for hospitalisation
- **Eligibility**: Identified through SECC data (automatically included)
- **How to use**: Visit any empanelled hospital with Aadhaar card
- **Website**: pmjay.gov.in
- **Relevance**: DV victims needing medical treatment, senior citizens

### Arogya Karnataka / Karnataka Arogya Bhagya
- **What**: State health insurance scheme
- **Benefit**: Cashless treatment at empanelled hospitals
- **Eligibility**: BPL families, APL families with income below ₹1.5 lakh
- **How to apply**: Through Suvarna Arogya Suraksha Trust
- **Relevance**: All crisis categories

### Free Treatment at Government Hospitals
- **What**: All government hospitals provide free emergency treatment
- **No documents needed** for emergency treatment
- **MLC (Medico-Legal Certificate)** is prepared free of charge if violence-related
- Includes: PHC, CHC, Taluk Hospital, District Hospital, and teaching hospitals

## 4. Financial Support Schemes

### PM Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana (PMGKAY)
- **What**: Free food grains
- **Benefit**: 5 kg per person per month (rice, wheat, coarse grains) — FREE
- **Eligibility**: All ration card holders (NFSA beneficiaries)
- **How to access**: Nearest Fair Price Shop with ration card + Aadhaar
- **Relevance**: All crisis categories — immediate food security

### Annapurna Scheme
- **What**: Food security for destitute senior citizens
- **Benefit**: 10 kg food grains per month — FREE
- **Eligibility**: Senior citizens (65+) who are not covered under IGNOAPS
- **How to apply**: Through Gram Panchayat / Ward Office

### Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension (IGNOAPS)
- **What**: Monthly pension for BPL senior citizens
- **Benefit**: ₹200/month (Central) + ₹400/month (Karnataka top-up) = ₹600/month
- **Eligibility**: 60+ years, BPL
- **How to apply**: Through Gram Panchayat / Ward Office

### Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension
- **What**: Monthly pension for BPL widows
- **Benefit**: ₹300/month (Central) + State top-up
- **Eligibility**: Widows aged 40-79, BPL
- **How to apply**: Through Gram Panchayat / Ward Office

### PM Kisan Samman Nidhi
- **What**: Income support for farmer families
- **Benefit**: ₹6,000/year in 3 installments
- **Eligibility**: Landholding farmer families
- **How to apply**: Through Common Service Centre or pmkisan.gov.in

## 5. Schemes for Senior Citizens

### Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS)
- **What**: Government-backed savings instrument
- **Benefit**: 8.2% annual interest (highest among government schemes)
- **Eligibility**: Citizens 60+ (55+ for retired defence/government employees)
- **Investment**: ₹1,000 to ₹30,00,000
- **Where**: Any post office or bank

### Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana (RVY)
- **What**: Free assistive devices for senior citizens
- **Benefit**: Hearing aids, walking sticks, spectacles, wheelchairs, dentures — FREE
- **Eligibility**: BPL senior citizens (60+)
- **How to apply**: Through District Social Welfare Office
- **Implementing agency**: ALIMCO

### Pradhan Mantri Vaya Vandana Yojana (PMVVY)
- **What**: Pension scheme for senior citizens
- **Benefit**: Guaranteed 7.4% annual return, monthly pension
- **Eligibility**: 60+ years
- **Investment**: Up to ₹15,00,000
- **Where**: LIC of India

### Varishtha Mediclaim (Health Insurance)
- **What**: Health insurance specifically for senior citizens
- **Available through**: National Insurance, United India, Oriental Insurance
- **Coverage**: Hospitalisation, pre/post hospital expenses
- **No pre-existing disease exclusion** after waiting period

## 6. Legal Aid Schemes

### KSLSA Legal Aid (Karnataka State Legal Services Authority)
- **What**: Free legal representation + court fee exemption
- **Eligibility**:
  - Annual income below ₹3,00,000
  - Women (regardless of income)
  - SC/ST members
  - Senior citizens
  - Disabled persons
  - Industrial workmen
  - Victims of mass disaster, trafficking, or ethnic violence
- **How to apply**: Visit DLSA at any district court complex
- **Contact**: 080-22113824

### NALSA Schemes
- **NALSA (Victims of Trafficking) Scheme**: Free legal aid for trafficking victims
- **NALSA (Legal Services to Disaster Victims) Scheme**: For natural disaster victims
- **NALSA (Child Friendly Legal Services) Scheme**: For children affected by crisis

## 7. How to Access These Schemes

### Single Window: Common Service Centre (CSC)
- Over 4,000 CSCs in Karnataka
- Can apply for most central government schemes
- Website: csc.gov.in
- Fee: Nominal (₹20-50 for most applications)

### District-Level Offices
- **District Collector's Office**: Coordinates all schemes
- **DWCD Office**: Women and child-related schemes
- **Social Welfare Department**: SC/ST, senior citizen, disability schemes
- **Revenue Department**: Land and property schemes
