# Domestic Violence — Complete Workflow (Step-by-Step)

> End-to-end guide for a DV survivor: from first safety steps to final court orders.

---

## Phase 0: Immediate Safety

### If You Are in Immediate Danger RIGHT NOW
1. **Call 112** (Emergency) or **100** (Police)
2. Go to the nearest **safe location** (neighbour, friend, family, temple/church/mosque)
3. If injured, go directly to any **government hospital** — treatment is FREE and cannot be refused
4. Call **181** (Women Helpline) — available 24/7

### Safety Planning
- Keep important documents in a bag ready to grab: Aadhaar, bank passbook, marriage certificate, children's birth certificates
- Memorize at least 2 emergency phone numbers
- Identify a safe person who can help (neighbour, relative, colleague)
- Keep some emergency cash hidden
- Save evidence: Take photos of injuries, screenshot threatening messages, save call recordings

## Phase 1: Report and Document

### Option A: Police (Criminal Route)
| Step | Action | Outcome |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Go to nearest police station | File FIR under BNS 85 (cruelty by husband/relatives) |
| 2 | If police refuse | Write complaint to SP + send copy by registered post |
| 3 | Medical examination | Get MLC (Medico-Legal Certificate) at hospital |
| 4 | Zero FIR | File at ANY police station if your area station refuses |

**BNS Sections for DV:**
- **S.85 (old IPC 498A)**: Cruelty by husband/relatives — up to 3 years + fine
- **S.74-76 (old IPC 323-326)**: Hurt/Grievous hurt — up to 7 years
- **S.78 (old IPC 354)**: Assault to outrage modesty — up to 5 years
- **S.351 (old IPC 506)**: Criminal intimidation — up to 7 years

### Option B: Protection Officer (Civil Route)
| Step | Action | Outcome |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Visit Protection Officer (District DWCD office) | File DIR (Domestic Incident Report) |
| 2 | PO prepares DIR Form I | Detailed record of all violence |
| 3 | DIR forwarded to Magistrate + Police SHO | Official record created |
| 4 | PO assists with Section 12 petition | Court process begins |

### Option C: Service Provider / NGO
- Any registered NGO under Section 10 of PWDVA can:
  - Record the incident
  - Provide shelter
  - Forward information to Magistrate/PO
  - Accompany you to court

### Do Both Routes Simultaneously
- You CAN file both FIR (police) AND Section 12 petition (court) at the same time
- They are independent — criminal case does NOT affect civil protection orders and vice versa

## Phase 2: Gather Evidence

### Types of Evidence (By Priority)

**Physical Evidence:**
- Medical reports / MLC from hospital
- Photos of injuries (with date stamp)
- Damaged property photos
- Torn clothes, broken items (preserve them)

**Documentary Evidence:**
- Marriage certificate
- Threatening messages (WhatsApp, SMS) — take screenshots + print
- Call recordings (admissible under BSA 2023 S.63 with certificate)
- Bank statements showing financial abuse (withdrawals, account freezes)
- Property documents
- Protection Officer's DIR

**Witness Evidence:**
- Statements from neighbours, friends, relatives
- Medical staff who treated you
- Protection Officer's report
- Counsellor's report (if you attended counselling)

### Electronic Evidence Requirements (BSA 2023 S.63)
- Print the digital evidence (screenshots, recordings)
- Attach a **Section 63 Certificate** stating:
  - The device used to generate the evidence
  - That the device was operating properly
  - That the content is authentic and unaltered
- Keep the original device safe — court may ask to examine it

## Phase 3: Court (Section 12 Petition)

### Filing the Petition
- File before the **Judicial Magistrate First Class** (JMFC) of the area where:
  - You reside, OR
  - The respondent resides, OR
  - The domestic violence occurred

### Reliefs You Can Request (All in One Petition)

| Relief | Section | What It Means |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Protection Order** | S.18 | Respondent prohibited from committing violence, contacting you, entering your workplace, etc. |
| **Residence Order** | S.19 | Right to live in the shared household; respondent can be removed; alternative accommodation |
| **Monetary Relief** | S.20 | Compensation for loss of earnings, medical expenses, property damage, maintenance |
| **Custody Order** | S.21 | Temporary custody of children |
| **Compensation** | S.22 | Damages for injuries and emotional harm |

### Court Timeline (What to Expect)

```
Day 0:  File Section 12 Petition + Interim Application
         ↓
Day 1-3: First Hearing (S.12(4) — within 3 days of filing)
         Magistrate may pass Interim Protection Order (ex parte)
         ↓
Day 15-30: Notice served on Respondent
           Respondent files reply
         ↓
Day 30-45: Arguments from both sides
           Evidence examination
         ↓
Day 60:  FINAL ORDER must be passed (S.12(5))
         (Courts often take longer — but this is the statutory deadline)
```

### Interim Protection Order (IPO)
- Can be passed **ex parte** (without hearing the respondent) in urgent cases
- Takes effect **immediately** upon passing
- Respondent must comply even before they are formally heard
- Breach of IPO = **criminal offence** (S.31) — imprisonment up to 1 year

## Phase 4: Shelter (If Needed)

### Options in Karnataka

| Type | Contact | Who It's For |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **One-Stop Centre (Sakhi)** | 181 | All women affected by violence |
| **Swadhar Greh** | District DWCD office | Women in difficult circumstances |
| **Short Stay Home** | DWCD | Temporary shelter (up to 3 years) |
| **NGO Shelters** | Various | Women + children |

### Your Rights in Shelter
- Under S.6 of PWDVA, you have the right to shelter
- The shelter home CANNOT turn you away for lack of documents
- Children can accompany you
- Shelter details are **confidential** — cannot be disclosed to the abuser

### Shared Household Rights (S.17)
- Even if the house is in your husband's name, you have the **right to reside** in the shared household
- You CANNOT be evicted from the shared household
- This applies to: wife, ex-wife, live-in partner, mother, sister, widow

## Phase 5: Legal Aid

### Who Qualifies for Free Legal Aid
Under Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 (Section 12):
- **Any woman** (regardless of income) — S.12(c)
- SC/ST members — S.12(b)
- Annual income below **₹3,00,000** (Karnataka ceiling) — S.12(h)
- Children under 18 — S.12(d)

### Where to Get Free Legal Aid
- **KSLSA** (Karnataka State Legal Services Authority): 080-22113824
- **DLSA** (District Legal Services Authority): At every district court complex
- **Taluk Legal Services Committee**: At every taluk court complex
- **Legal Aid Clinic**: At every court complex, universities

### What Free Legal Aid Includes
- Free lawyer for all court hearings
- Free drafting of petitions, applications, and replies
- Free mediation/conciliation services
- No court fee waived (DV petitions are already free)

## Phase 6: Enforcement

### If the Respondent Violates the Order

| Violation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Breach of Protection Order | File complaint under S.31 PWDVA → imprisonment up to 1 year |
| Non-payment of monetary relief | Execution application before same Magistrate → attachment of property/salary |
| Continued violence despite order | New FIR + contempt application |

### S.31 — Breach of Protection Order
- **Cognizable** — police can arrest without warrant
- **Non-bailable** — respondent must apply to court for bail
- Punishment: up to **1 year imprisonment** + fine up to ₹20,000
- The aggrieved person OR the Protection Officer can file the complaint

## Phase 7: Long-Term Steps

### Divorce (If Desired)
- File for divorce under Hindu Marriage Act S.13 / Special Marriage Act S.27 / relevant personal law
- DV can be ground for divorce (cruelty)
- Maintenance rights continue even after divorce under S.125 CrPC

### Maintenance
- **Under S.20 PWDVA**: Monetary relief as part of DV proceedings
- **Under S.125 CrPC (now S.144 BNSS)**: Independent maintenance claim
- **Under personal law**: Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act S.18

### Child Custody
- **Interim custody**: Under S.21 PWDVA during DV proceedings
- **Permanent custody**: Under Guardians and Wards Act before Family Court
- Children under 5 → generally with mother (tender years doctrine)
