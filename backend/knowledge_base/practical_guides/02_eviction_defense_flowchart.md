# Eviction Defense — Complete Workflow (Step-by-Step)

> End-to-end guide for tenants/owners facing illegal eviction in Karnataka.

---

## Phase 0: Are You Being Illegally Evicted?

### Quick Assessment

| Situation | Legal? | Your Action |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Landlord gives 15-day oral notice to vacate | ❌ Illegal | Need written notice (minimum 15 days for monthly tenancy under TPA S.106) |
| Landlord locks your house while you're away | ❌ Illegal | File FIR + civil suit for possession |
| Landlord cuts water/electricity | ❌ Illegal | File complaint to BESCOM/BWSSB + police |
| Landlord sends written 15-day notice, valid reason | ✅ Legal | You have right to challenge in court |
| Court order for eviction served | ✅ Legal | File appeal within 30 days |
| Family member throws you out of ancestral home | ❌ Illegal | File S.6 Specific Relief Act (dispossession suit) |
| Senior citizen evicted by children | ❌ Illegal | File before SDM Tribunal |
| Municipal demolition with notice | ⚠️ Check | Verify if proper procedure followed |

### Types of Eviction

| Type | Key Law | Forum |
|------|---------|-------|
| Tenant eviction | Karnataka Rent Act 1999 | Rent Tribunal / Civil Court |
| Owner dispossession | Specific Relief Act S.6 | Civil Court |
| Senior citizen eviction | Senior Citizens Act 2007 S.23 | SDM Tribunal |
| Municipal demolition | BBMP Act / KMC Act | Civil Court + High Court |
| Bank/loan default | SARFAESI Act | DRT / Civil Court |

## Phase 1: Immediate Steps (Day 0)

### If You Are Being Physically Thrown Out RIGHT NOW

1. **Call 112** (Police Emergency)
2. Record video/photos of what's happening (on your phone)
3. Identify witnesses (neighbours, passersby)
4. DO NOT use physical force to resist — call police instead
5. If police arrive, ask them to make a **Panchnama** (spot report)

### If You Were Locked Out While Away

1. Go to the **nearest police station** — file an FIR:
   - **BNS S.329** (Criminal trespass) — if they entered your premises
   - **BNS S.126** (Wrongful restraint) — if they're preventing your entry
   - **BNS S.303** (Mischief causing damage) — if property damaged
2. If police refuse FIR: Write complaint to **SP/DCP** + send by registered post
3. Take photos of: locked door, changed locks, your belongings inside (if visible through window)

### Document Everything
- Date and time of eviction/attempted eviction
- Who was present (names, photos if safe)
- Any threats made (record if possible)
- Damage to your property
- Your rent receipts, agreement, payment proof

## Phase 2: Police Complaint

### Filing the FIR

**Go to**: The police station having jurisdiction over the property address.

**Relevant BNS Sections:**

| Section | Offence | Punishment |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| BNS S.329 (old IPC 441) | Criminal trespass | Up to 3 months + fine |
| BNS S.331 (old IPC 443) | Lurking house-trespass | Up to 2 years + fine |
| BNS S.126 (old IPC 341) | Wrongful restraint | Up to 1 month + ₹5,000 fine |
| BNS S.127 (old IPC 342) | Wrongful confinement | Up to 1 year + fine |
| BNS S.303 (old IPC 425) | Mischief causing damage | Up to 3 months + fine |
| BNS S.351 (old IPC 506) | Criminal intimidation | Up to 2 years + fine |

### If Police Refuse

1. **Write to SP/DCP** of the district by registered post
2. File complaint with **Superintendent of Police** office
3. Approach **Judicial Magistrate** directly under BNSS S.175(3) — Magistrate can direct police to register FIR
4. File **Zero FIR** at ANY police station — they must register and transfer to jurisdictional station

## Phase 3: Legal Action (Civil Court)

### Option A: Suit for Possession (Specific Relief Act S.6)

**When to use**: You were dispossessed from property you were in possession of.

**Key Features:**
- No need to prove ownership — only **prior possession** matters
- Must be filed **within 6 months** of dispossession
- Court ONLY looks at who was in possession, NOT who owns the property
- Very powerful for tenants locked out by landlords

**What to include in the suit:**
- Description of property
- Your possession details (since when, rent agreement, utility bills)
- How you were dispossessed
- Prayer for restoration of possession

**Court fee**: Ad valorem on the value of the property (but for possession suits, typically ₹200-1,000)

### Option B: Interim Injunction (CPC Order XXXIX Rule 1 & 2)

**When to use**: You're still in possession but landlord is threatening/attempting eviction.

**Key Features:**
- **Temporary restraining order** against the landlord
- Can be obtained on the **first hearing** itself (even ex parte)
- Landlord is prohibited from evicting you until the court decides the case
- **Three tests** court applies:
  1. Prima facie case (you have a reasonable argument)
  2. Balance of convenience (more harmful to evict you than to wait)
  3. Irreparable injury (you'll suffer loss that money can't compensate)

**How to file:**
1. File main suit (for injunction/possession)
2. File **Interlocutory Application (IA)** for interim injunction along with main suit
3. Request urgent hearing
4. Court can pass interim order on the same day if urgency is shown

### Option C: Karnataka Rent Act 1999 (For Tenants)

**When to use**: You rent property in a notified area of Karnataka.

**Key Features:**
- Landlord can ONLY evict on **specified grounds** (S.21):
  - Non-payment of rent
  - Subletting without consent
  - Nuisance/damage to property
  - Landlord's bona fide personal need
  - Property requires demolition/repair
- Even for valid grounds, landlord must follow **proper procedure**:
  1. Written notice
  2. Filing petition before Rent Tribunal
  3. Proving the ground before the Tribunal
- **Tenant has 15 days** to remedy (pay rent, stop nuisance) after notice

## Phase 4: Evidence Collection

### Priority Evidence for Eviction Cases

| Evidence | How to Get It | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Rent agreement | Your copy / registered copy from SRO | Proves tenancy + terms |
| Rent receipts / bank transfers | Your records / bank statement | Proves you paid rent |
| Utility bills in your name | BESCOM/BWSSB office | Proves possession |
| Aadhaar with property address | UIDAI | Proves residence |
| Voter ID with property address | Electoral office | Proves residence |
| Photos/videos of eviction | Your phone | Proves illegal eviction |
| Witness statements | Neighbours, friends | Corroboration |
| Police complaint copy | Police station | Shows you reported |
| Property tax receipts | BBMP/municipality | Proves ownership (if owner) |
| Legal notice sent by landlord | Your copy / lawyer | Shows landlord's claim |

### If You Lost Documents During Eviction
- Bank statements: Available from bank (free for last 1 year)
- Aadhaar: Reprint from mAadhaar app or UIDAI website
- Rent agreement: If registered → get certified copy from Sub-Registrar
- Utility bills: Available from BESCOM/BWSSB customer portal

## Phase 5: Court Hearing

### What Happens at Court

**First Hearing (1-7 days after filing):**
- Judge verifies petition completeness
- If you filed interim application → Judge may hear urgently
- Judge may pass **ex parte interim order** (without hearing landlord) if urgency shown
- Issues notice to landlord (15-30 day return date)

**Subsequent Hearings:**
- Landlord files written statement/reply
- Both sides argue
- Evidence is examined
- Judge passes order

### Expected Timeline

| Stage | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Filing to first hearing | 1-7 days |
| Interim order (if urgent) | 1-3 days |
| Notice to respondent | 15-30 days |
| Full hearing | 3-6 months |
| Final judgment | 6-12 months |
| Appeal (if any) | Additional 6-12 months |

## Phase 6: Enforcement

### If You Win the Case

| Order | How to Enforce |
|-------|---------------|
| Possession order | Apply for execution → court bailiff physically restores possession |
| Injunction order | If violated → file contempt application → respondent can be jailed |
| Damages/costs awarded | Apply for execution → attachment of respondent's property/bank account |

### If the Landlord Violates Court Order
1. File **contempt of court** application before the same court
2. Punishment: Fine + imprisonment (up to 6 months under Contempt of Courts Act)
3. File new FIR if criminal acts committed during violation

## Phase 7: Legal Aid

### Free Legal Aid for Eviction Cases

**Who qualifies:**
- Annual income below ₹3,00,000 (Karnataka limit)
- SC/ST members
- Women
- Senior citizens
- Disabled persons
- Industrial workmen

**Where to go:**
- **DLSA** (District Legal Services Authority) at every district court complex
- **KSLSA**: 080-22113824
- **Taluk Legal Services Committee** at every taluk court

## Decision Tree

```
START: Are you being evicted?
  ├── Currently being physically evicted?
  │   ├── YES → Call 112 → File FIR → File S.6 suit within 6 months
  │   └── NO → Continue below
  │
  ├── Are you a tenant?
  │   ├── YES → Check: Did landlord give written notice with valid reason?
  │   │   ├── NO valid reason → File injunction + challenge eviction
  │   │   └── YES valid reason → Check: Did you get chance to remedy?
  │   │       ├── NO → File before Rent Tribunal → eviction order is void
  │   │       └── YES → Argue before Rent Tribunal → hire lawyer
  │   └── NO → Continue below
  │
  ├── Are you an owner/co-owner?
  │   ├── YES → File S.6 suit (possession) + injunction
  │   └── Continue below
  │
  ├── Are you a senior citizen (60+)?
  │   ├── YES → File before SDM Tribunal (free) → S.23 property restoration
  │   └── Continue below
  │
  └── Are you a family member in shared household?
      ├── DV victim → File S.12 petition under PWDVA + S.19 residence order
      └── Other → File civil suit for injunction/possession
```
