# Senior Citizen Protection — Complete Workflow

> End-to-end guide for senior citizens facing neglect, abuse, or property disputes in Karnataka.

---

## Phase 0: Quick Assessment — What Is Your Situation?

| Situation | Primary Law | Where to Go |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Children refuse to give food/medicine/shelter | Senior Citizens Act S.5 | SDM Tribunal |
| Children took your property and now neglect you | Senior Citizens Act S.23 | SDM Tribunal |
| Children physically beating/abusing you | BNS S.85 + Senior Citizens Act S.24 | Police + SDM |
| Being evicted from your own home by children | Senior Citizens Act S.23 + S.6 SRA | SDM + Civil Court |
| Tenant evicting you (senior citizen) | Karnataka Rent Act 1999 | Rent Tribunal |
| Children took pension/money without consent | BNS S.316 (criminal breach of trust) | Police |
| Children forged your property documents | BNS S.336-338 (forgery) | Police |
| Need maintenance from children | Senior Citizens Act S.5 | SDM Tribunal |

## Phase 1: Immediate Steps

### If You Are in Danger
1. **Call 112** (Emergency) or **1800-180-1551** (Elder Helpline, toll-free)
2. If physically hurt → go to any **government hospital** (free treatment)
3. Contact **nearest police station** if children are violent

### Secure Your Documents
Keep the following safe (with a trusted person if necessary):
- Property documents (sale deed, gift deed, settlement deed)
- Bank passbook and cheque book
- Aadhaar card, Voter ID
- Pension papers
- Will (if any)
- Medical records

> **Warning**: If your children have access to your bank account or property documents, consider:
> - Informing the bank NOT to allow withdrawals without your physical presence
> - Getting certified copies of property documents from Sub-Registrar
> - Revoking any Power of Attorney given to children

## Phase 2: File for Maintenance (SDM Tribunal)

### Who Can File
- Any parent (including adoptive parent) aged 60+
- Who is unable to maintain themselves from their own earnings/property
- Against children, grandchildren, or relatives in possession of their property

### How to File

1. **Write a simple application** (in Kannada or English):
   - Your name, age, address
   - Names of children you're filing against
   - What you need (maintenance amount, property restoration)
   - Brief facts of neglect/abuse
   
2. **Submit to SDM office** of your taluk
   - No court fee required (completely FREE)
   - No lawyer needed (but you can bring one)
   - Can be filed by registered post if you can't travel

3. **Timeline**:
   - Notice to children: 15 days
   - Interim maintenance order: Within 30 days
   - Final order: Within 90 days (statutory deadline)

### Maintenance Amount
- Maximum **₹10,000/month** per applicant
- Divided among all children based on their ability to pay
- Payable from the **date of application** (not date of order)
- If multiple children: each pays their proportionate share

## Phase 3: Property Restoration (S.23)

### When This Applies
- You transferred property to a child/relative (by gift, sale, settlement deed)
- The transfer was conditional on them providing maintenance/basic amenities
- They are NOT providing what was agreed

### What the Law Says
- Such transfer is **deemed VOID** under S.23(1)
- The Tribunal (SDM) can order the property restored to you
- The child/relative must return possession

### Evidence That Helps
- Original transfer deed (gift deed, sale deed, settlement deed)
- Witnesses to the condition of maintenance
- Any written agreement about maintenance
- Bank statements showing you stopped receiving money
- Photos of your living conditions
- Neighbours' statements about neglect

### Important: 2019 Amendment
- The 2019 Amendment to the Senior Citizens Act **strengthened** S.23
- Now explicitly covers ALL types of transfers (not just gifts)
- Includes property transferred by will that takes effect after death
- Children CANNOT claim that the transfer was unconditional without proving it

## Phase 4: Criminal Action (If Needed)

### When to Involve Police

| Situation | BNS Section | Punishment |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Physical assault by children | S.115 (Voluntarily causing hurt) | Up to 1 year + fine |
| Threatening you | S.351 (Criminal intimidation) | Up to 2 years + fine |
| Stealing your money/valuables | S.303 (Theft) | Up to 3 years + fine |
| Misusing your bank account | S.316 (Criminal breach of trust) | Up to 3 years + fine |
| Forging your property documents | S.336 (Forgery) | Up to 2 years + fine |
| Abandonment | Senior Citizens Act S.24 | Up to 3 months + ₹5,000 fine |

### S.24 — Abandonment (Special Provision)
- **Cognizable** offence — police can arrest without warrant
- **Non-bailable** — must apply to court for bail
- Applies when children/relatives abandon a senior citizen
- Punishment: Fine up to ₹5,000 OR imprisonment up to 3 months OR both

## Phase 5: Shelter and Old Age Homes

### Government Options in Karnataka

| Facility | Where | Contact |
|----------|-------|---------|
| State-run Old Age Homes | Every district (S.19 mandate) | District DWCD Office |
| BBMP Senior Citizen Centres | Bengaluru | BBMP Help Desk |
| Karnataka Vruddha Ashrama Yojana | State-wide | Social Welfare Department |

### Your Rights
- Under S.19 of the Senior Citizens Act, the **State Government must** establish and maintain old age homes
- At least **one old age home** in every district (capacity: minimum 150)
- Admission is free for destitute senior citizens
- Medical care, food, and basic amenities must be provided

### Private Options
- HelpAge India shelters: 1800-180-1253
- Various NGO-run homes (check with DWCD office for registered list)
- Paid senior citizen communities and retirement homes

## Phase 6: Government Schemes for Senior Citizens — Karnataka

### Financial Support

| Scheme | Benefit | Eligibility |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Sandhya Suraksha Yojana** | ₹600/month pension | BPL senior citizens (60+) |
| **Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension (IGNOAPS)** | ₹200/month (Central) + ₹400/month (State top-up) | BPL, 60+ |
| **National Family Benefit Scheme** | ₹20,000 lump sum | On death of breadwinner (18-59 years) |

### Healthcare

| Scheme | Benefit | How to Access |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY)** | ₹5 lakh/year health cover | Through government hospital |
| **RSBY (Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana)** | Cashless treatment | BPL families |
| **Karnataka Arogya Bhagya** | Free treatment at empanelled hospitals | BPL families |
| **CGHS** (for retired govt employees) | Comprehensive health coverage | Government pensioners |

### Other Benefits
- **Free KSRTC bus travel** for citizens 65+ (with senior citizen ID)
- **Priority at government offices** and banks
- **Income tax benefits**: Higher basic exemption (₹3,00,000 for 60-80; ₹5,00,000 for 80+)
- **Reverse mortgage**: Available through banks for property owners 60+
- **Concession on property tax**: 50% rebate in BBMP areas for properties self-occupied by senior citizens

## Phase 7: Legal Aid

### Free Legal Aid
Under Legal Services Authorities Act S.12:
- **All senior citizens** qualify for free legal aid (regardless of income) — S.12(f)
- Contact DLSA at the nearest district court complex
- Or KSLSA: 080-22113824

### What Free Legal Aid Covers
- Free lawyer for SDM Tribunal proceedings
- Free drafting of applications and petitions
- Free mediation services
- Representation in appeals

## Phase 8: Long-Term Protection

### Will and Estate Planning
- Make a **registered Will** to ensure property goes to the desired person
- Consider creating a **trust** if property is substantial
- Keep certified copies of all property documents
- Inform the Sub-Registrar's office of any encumbrances

### Revoking Previous Transfers
- If you gave property to children and they're neglecting you:
  - File S.23 application at SDM Tribunal
  - Also consider filing a civil suit for cancellation of transfer deed
  - If Power of Attorney was given → execute a **revocation deed** and register it

### Bank Account Protection
- Use a **nominee** on all accounts
- Consider a **joint account** with a trusted person (not the abuser)
- Set up **transaction alerts** on your phone
- Do NOT share ATM PIN or internet banking passwords

## Decision Tree

```
START: Senior citizen (60+) needs help
  ├── Physically in danger?
  │   ├── YES → Call 112 → Hospital → FIR → SDM Tribunal
  │   └── NO → Continue below
  │
  ├── Not receiving maintenance from children?
  │   ├── YES → File application at SDM Tribunal (free)
  │   │   ├── Need money urgently? → Ask for interim order (30 days)
  │   │   └── Can wait? → Full hearing (90 days)
  │   └── NO → Continue below
  │
  ├── Property taken by children?
  │   ├── YES → File S.23 application at SDM Tribunal
  │   │   └── Also file FIR if documents forged
  │   └── NO → Continue below
  │
  ├── Being evicted from home?
  │   ├── By children → SDM Tribunal + S.6 civil suit
  │   ├── By tenant/landlord → Rent Tribunal / Civil Court
  │   └── By bank (loan default) → DRT + legal aid
  │
  └── Need shelter?
      ├── Government old age home → District DWCD Office
      ├── HelpAge India → 1800-180-1253
      └── Private options → Check local listings
```
