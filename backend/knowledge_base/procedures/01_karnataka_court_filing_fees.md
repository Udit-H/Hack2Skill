# Karnataka Court Filing Procedures and Fees

## Where to File — Court Types

### 1. Metropolitan Magistrate / JMFC (Judicial Magistrate First Class)
**For**: DV cases (protection orders), criminal complaints (FIR-related), BNS offences.
**Location in Bangalore**: City Criminal Court Complex, Nrupathunga Road.

### 2. City Civil Court / Small Causes Court
**For**: Eviction matters, rent disputes, injunction suits, property disputes below ₹20 lakhs.
**Location in Bangalore**: City Civil Court Complex, Nrupathunga Road.

### 3. District Court / Principal Civil Judge
**For**: Property suits above ₹20 lakhs, execution petitions, higher-value injunctions.
**Location in Bangalore**: District Courts, near Nrupathunga Road / Mayo Hall area.

### 4. Family Court
**For**: Divorce, maintenance under S.125 CrPC, custody matters.
**Location in Bangalore**: Family Court Complex, Shantinagar.

### 5. Rent Controller
**For**: Eviction petitions under Karnataka Rent Act.
**In Bangalore**: Court of Small Causes (same as City Civil Court Complex).

### 6. Senior Citizens Maintenance Tribunal
**For**: Maintenance applications under Senior Citizens Act 2007.
**In Bangalore**: Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) office. Sub-Divisional offices at Bangalore North and Bangalore South.

## Court Fees in Karnataka

### DV Cases (Under PWDVA 2005)
- **Application for protection order**: NO court fee. Filed before Magistrate.
- **Affidavit stamp paper**: ₹20 non-judicial stamp paper.
- **Certified copies**: Free under Legal Aid. Otherwise ₹5-10 per page.
- **Total typical cost with legal aid**: ₹0 (completely free).
- **Total typical cost without legal aid**: ₹200-500 (stamp paper + copies + notarization).

### Eviction / Rent Matters
- **Injunction application**: ₹100-500 (based on Karnataka Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act).
- **Eviction suit**: Ad valorem based on annual rent. Typically ₹200-500.
- **Temporary injunction (Order XXXIX CPC)**: ₹100-200 additional.
- **Affidavit**: ₹20 stamp paper.
- **Vakalatnama (power of attorney to lawyer)**: ₹20.
- **Process fee for notice**: ₹50-100.
- **Total typical cost**: ₹500-2,000.

### Senior Citizens Tribunal
- **Maintenance application**: NO court fee. Free of charge.
- **No stamp paper required for application.**
- **Total cost**: ₹0.

### Criminal Complaints
- **Private complaint under BNS**: ₹100 (approximately).
- **FIR at police station**: FREE. No charge.

## Stamp Paper and Affidavit Requirements

### Non-Judicial Stamp Paper
- ₹20 stamp paper: For affidavits in most courts (civil and criminal).
- ₹50 stamp paper: For agreements, undertakings submitted to court.
- ₹100 stamp paper: For power of attorney (General Power of Attorney is higher).
- Available at: Licensed stamp vendors near court complexes, or e-stamp centers.

### Affidavit Format
Standard affidavit format for Karnataka courts:
1. Heading: Name of court, case number (if known).
2. Body: "I, [Name], [Age], [Occupation], resident of [Address], do hereby solemnly affirm and state as follows:"
3. Numbered paragraphs stating facts.
4. Declaration: "I state that the facts stated above are true and correct to the best of my knowledge and belief."
5. Deponent's signature.
6. Notarization: Sworn before Notary Public or Oaths Commissioner.

### Where to Get Notarization
- **Notary Public**: Available near every court complex. Charge: ₹50-100 per affidavit.
- **Oaths Commissioner**: Appointed by High Court. Available at court complexes.
- **Court itself**: Magistrate can administer oath if affidavit is for case before that court.

## Filing Procedure

### Step 1: Draft Petition/Application
- Use proper format (or use Sahayak to generate).
- Include required affidavit.
- Prepare sufficient copies (minimum 3: court, respondent, self).

### Step 2: Court Fees
- Purchase court fee stamp of required denomination from stamp counter at court.
- Affix on first page of petition.

### Step 3: Filing Counter
- Submit petition at filing counter of appropriate court.
- Counter clerk checks format, court fee, copies.
- If accepted, petition is numbered and date-stamped.

### Step 4: Case Number
- Case number assigned (format: CASE-TYPE/YEAR/NUMBER).
- Date of first hearing communicated.

### Step 5: Service on Respondent
- Court orders notice/summons to respondent.
- Can be served through court process server, registered post, or in some cases publication.

## E-Courts / Online Filing in Karnataka

### E-Filing Portal
- Website: efiling.ecourts.gov.in
- Registration required (Aadhaar-based).
- Currently available for: Supreme Court, some High Courts.
- For District Courts in Karnataka: Limited e-filing available. Check with specific court.

### Case Status Check
- Website: ecourts.gov.in
- Select State: Karnataka → District: Bangalore Urban.
- Search by case number, party name, or advocate name.
- Free of charge.

### Daily Cause List
- Available on ecourts.gov.in.
- Shows which cases are listed for hearing on which date.

## Karnataka High Court Civil Rules — Key Provisions

### Chapter III: Pleadings
- All pleadings must be in English or Kannada.
- Typed/printed on legal-size paper.
- Paragraphs numbered consecutively.
- Documents relied upon to be listed.
- Dates of events to be chronological.

### Chapter V: Filing
- Original and sufficient copies for all parties.
- Index of documents annexed.
- Vakalatnama (if filed through advocate).
- Court fee stamps affixed.
- Address for service mentioned.

### Number of Copies Required
- **Original**: For court record.
- **One copy per respondent**: For service on each respondent/opposite party.
- **One copy for self**: For your record.
- **Extra copy**: For court library (in High Court matters).
- Typically: **Minimum 3 copies (court + respondent + self).**
