# Police Complaint Procedures — FIR, NCR, and Zero FIR

## Types of Police Complaints

### FIR (First Information Report)
- Filed for **cognizable offences** (where police can arrest without warrant and investigate without Magistrate's permission).
- Cognizable offences include: assault causing hurt, criminal intimidation, cruelty by husband (BNS S.85), house trespass, theft, robbery, etc.
- FIR is **mandatory** — police MUST register it if the offence reported is cognizable.

### NCR (Non-Cognizable Report)
- Filed for **non-cognizable offences** (where police need Magistrate's permission to investigate).
- Non-cognizable offences include: simple hurt, simple assault, defamation, cheating (in some cases).
- Police record the complaint but cannot investigate without Magistrate's order.
- Complainant can approach Magistrate for direction to police to investigate.

### Zero FIR
- FIR registered at **any police station**, regardless of jurisdiction.
- The police station receiving the Zero FIR forwards it to the police station having actual jurisdiction.
- **Key benefit**: You can file FIR at the nearest police station without worrying about which station has jurisdiction. Saves time in emergency.
- Introduced under BNSS 2023 (Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita), Section 173(1).

## How to File an FIR

### Step 1: Go to Police Station
- Go to the nearest police station (Zero FIR allows any station).
- You can also call 100 (Police) or 112 (Emergency) first.

### Step 2: Give Information
- Give oral or written information about the offence.
- Clearly describe: what happened, when, where, who did it, injuries if any.
- Police officer (SHO or duty officer) MUST record your complaint.

### Step 3: FIR Is Written
- Police officer writes the FIR in a standard format.
- FIR contents: name of informant, date/time of incident, description of incident, names of accused (if known), section of law applicable.
- FIR is read back to you. You sign it.

### Step 4: Get Your Copy
- **You are entitled to a free copy of the FIR.** This is your legal right.
- FIR copy is important evidence. Keep it safe.
- FIR is also sent to Magistrate.

### Step 5: Investigation Begins
- Police investigate the case.
- They may: visit scene, record statements, collect evidence, arrest accused (if cognizable and arrest is warranted).

## What If Police Refuse to Register FIR

### This is illegal. Police MUST register FIR for cognizable offences.

### Remedy 1: Written Complaint to SP/Commissioner
- Write complaint to Superintendent of Police (SP) or Commissioner of Police.
- In Bangalore: Commissioner of Police, Bangalore City.
- SP/Commissioner can direct the SHO to register FIR.

### Remedy 2: Section 173(3) BNSS — Complaint to Magistrate
- Send written complaint by registered post to Superintendent of Police.
- If no action within 15 days, approach Magistrate.
- Magistrate can direct police to register FIR and investigate.

### Remedy 3: Direct Complaint to Magistrate (Section 200 CrPC / Section 223 BNSS)
- File a private complaint directly before the Magistrate.
- Magistrate can take cognizance of the offence and direct investigation.
- This bypasses police entirely.

### Remedy 4: Online FIR
- Karnataka Police allows online complaint registration for certain offences.
- Website: ksp.karnataka.gov.in
- You can file complaint online and track status.

### Remedy 5: Human Rights Commission
- If police refuse and there is custodial violence or discrimination, complain to Karnataka State Human Rights Commission.

## FIR vs DIR (Domestic Incident Report) — Key Differences

| Feature | FIR | DIR (Form I under PWDVA) |
|---------|-----|--------------------------|
| Purpose | Criminal prosecution | Civil protection under DV Act |
| Filed at | Police station | Police station, Protection Officer, or service provider |
| Result | Criminal investigation, possible arrest | Protection order, residence order, monetary relief from Magistrate |
| Nature | Criminal proceeding | Civil proceeding |
| Can file both? | **YES — you can file BOTH FIR and DIR simultaneously** |

### When to File FIR (for DV cases)
- When there is physical injury (BNS S.115 — hurt).
- When there are threats to kill (BNS S.329 — criminal intimidation).
- When there is cruelty by husband/relatives (BNS S.85).
- When there is sexual assault.

### When to File DIR (for DV cases)
- For all types of domestic violence (physical, sexual, emotional, economic).
- To get protection order from Magistrate.
- To get residence order (right to stay in shared household).
- To get monetary relief/maintenance.

### Recommended Approach for DV
**File BOTH FIR and DIR.** They serve different purposes:
- FIR → Criminal case → Punishment for accused.
- DIR → Civil remedy → Protection order, maintenance, residence order for victim.

## Practical Tips for Filing FIR in Bangalore

### Which Police Station
- File at the police station having jurisdiction over the place where offence occurred.
- Or file Zero FIR at nearest station.
- Bangalore has multiple police stations organized by sub-divisions (law & order stations) and Crime Investigation wings.

### What to Bring
- ID proof (Aadhaar, driving license, passport — any one).
- Medical reports if injured (go to hospital first for MLC).
- Any evidence: photos, screenshots of threats, witness details.
- Written complaint if possible (helps ensure everything is recorded accurately).

### Women's Help Desk
- Most police stations in Bangalore have a Women's Help Desk.
- Women police officers available for DV, sexual harassment, and other cases involving women.
- You have the right to request a woman officer.

### Timeline
- FIR registration: Should be immediate. Cannot be delayed.
- Investigation: No statutory time limit for completion, but Magistrate monitors progress.
- Chargesheet: Filed in court after investigation.
