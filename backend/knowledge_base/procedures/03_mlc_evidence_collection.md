# Medico-Legal Certificate (MLC) and Medical Evidence Collection

## What is an MLC

A Medico-Legal Certificate (MLC) is a medical document prepared by a doctor after examining a patient whose injury/illness has legal implications. It serves as expert evidence in court.

## When to Get an MLC

### Domestic Violence Cases
- After any physical assault/injury by husband or relatives.
- Even if injuries seem minor — document everything.
- Get MLC immediately after incident (do not delay).

### Eviction Violence
- If landlord or landlord's agents physically assaulted you.
- If injured during illegal eviction attempt.

### Senior Citizen Abuse
- Physical injuries from abuse by children/relatives.
- Evidence of neglect (malnutrition, untreated medical conditions).

## How to Get an MLC in Karnataka

### Step 1: Go to Hospital
- Go to nearest **government hospital** — they CANNOT refuse MLC.
- Government hospitals in Bangalore: Victoria Hospital, Bowring Hospital, K.C. General Hospital, Vani Vilas Hospital (women), Jayanagar General Hospital.
- Private hospitals can also provide MLC but government hospital MLCs carry more weight.

### Step 2: Registration
- Register at OPD/casualty/emergency.
- Tell them you need MLC (Medico-Legal Case).
- Some hospitals have separate MLC counter.

### Step 3: Medical Examination
- Doctor examines and documents all injuries.
- Doctor records: type of injury, size, location on body, possible cause, weapon used (if identifiable), age of injury (fresh/old).
- Doctor may also note patient's mental state/emotional condition.

### Step 4: MLC Document
The MLC contains:
1. Patient details (name, age, sex, address).
2. Date and time of examination.
3. Date and time of incident (as reported by patient).
4. History of incident (as narrated by patient).
5. Description of injuries — each injury separately described with:
   - Nature (abrasion, bruise, laceration, fracture, burn).
   - Size (in centimeters).
   - Location on body.
   - Color (indicates age of injury).
   - Type of weapon/force likely used.
6. Opinion on nature of injuries (simple/grievous).
7. Whether injuries are consistent with the history given.
8. Advice: Further treatment needed, X-rays, admission, etc.
9. Doctor's signature, name, designation, hospital stamp.

### Step 5: Get Copies
- Get at least 2 copies of MLC.
- One for your record, one for court/police.
- Hospital also sends copy to police if they registered MLC.

## Important Points About MLC

### Hospital CANNOT Refuse
Government hospitals are legally bound to provide MLC. Refusing is a punishable offence.

### No Police Complaint Required First
You do NOT need to file FIR before getting MLC. Go to hospital first, file FIR later.
In fact, getting MLC first is better — it preserves medical evidence.

### Free at Government Hospitals
MLC examination at government hospitals is FREE.

### Timing Matters
- Get MLC as soon as possible after incident.
- Fresh injuries are easier to document and more credible.
- Delay in getting MLC can be questioned by defense in court.
- But: even delayed MLC is better than no MLC.

### Photograph Injuries Too
- Take photographs before, during, and after medical examination.
- Photos supplement MLC but do not replace it.

## Evidence Collection Checklist for DV Cases

### Immediate (Within Hours)
- [ ] Get MLC from hospital.
- [ ] Photograph all injuries (with date reference).
- [ ] Save threatening messages (WhatsApp, SMS).
- [ ] Note names of witnesses (neighbors who heard/saw).
- [ ] File FIR at police station.
- [ ] Contact Protection Officer or service provider for DIR.

### Short Term (Within Days)
- [ ] Get certified copies of MLC.
- [ ] File application before Magistrate (Form II under DV Rules).
- [ ] Secure important documents (marriage certificate, Aadhaar, children's documents).
- [ ] Gather financial evidence (bank statements, salary slips of respondent).
- [ ] Collect property documents (rent agreement, ownership docs).

### For Trial
- [ ] All WhatsApp/SMS screenshots with Section 63 BSA certificate.
- [ ] Medical records and follow-up treatment records.
- [ ] Witness affidavits.
- [ ] Previous complaints/FIRs.
- [ ] Photographs with verification.
- [ ] Financial documents showing dependency/economic abuse.

## How Medical Evidence Helps in Court

### Corroboration
MLC corroborates the victim's testimony about physical violence. Even in sole-testimony cases (Section 32 PWDVA), MLC significantly strengthens the case.

### Nature of Injuries
- **Simple injuries**: Establish hurt (BNS S.115) — up to 1 year imprisonment.
- **Grievous injuries**: Establish grievous hurt (BNS S.117) — up to 7 years.
- **Injuries with weapons**: Establish use of dangerous weapons (BNS S.118) — up to 3 years.

### Pattern of Abuse
Multiple MLCs over time establish a pattern of repeated domestic violence, which is crucial for:
- Proving "cruelty" under BNS S.85.
- Getting protection order under PWDVA S.18.
- Higher compensation under PWDVA S.22.

## Right to Information Act, 2005 — For Getting Records

### What You Can Get
- Police records (FIR copies, investigation status).
- Hospital records (MLC copies, treatment records).
- Government scheme eligibility documents.
- Court records (if lost).
- Any government-held information.

### How to File RTI Application
1. Write application to PIO (Public Information Officer) of relevant department.
2. Pay ₹10 (by cash/Indian Postal Order/demand draft).
3. BPL applicants: No fee.
4. Response: Within 30 days (10 days if related to life/liberty).
5. If refused: Appeal to First Appellate Authority, then Information Commission.
