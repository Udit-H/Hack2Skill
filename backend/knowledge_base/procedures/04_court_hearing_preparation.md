# Court Hearing Preparation Guide — Karnataka

> Practical guide for first-time litigants appearing before Karnataka courts.

---

## 1. Types of Courts You May Visit

| Court | Jurisdiction | Where |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Magistrate Court** | FIRs, Protection Orders (DV Act S.12), maintenance orders | District/Taluk HQ |
| **Civil Judge (Junior Division)** | Injunctions, eviction suits up to ₹5 lakh | District/Taluk HQ |
| **Civil Judge (Senior Division)** | Suits ₹5 lakh – ₹20 lakh | District Court Complex |
| **District & Sessions Court** | Criminal appeals, DV appeals, suits > ₹20 lakh | District HQ |
| **Family Court** | Divorce, custody, maintenance, DV petitions | Bengaluru, Mysuru, etc. |
| **Senior Citizen Tribunal** | Maintenance under S.5, property restoration under S.23 | SDM office |
| **Rent Tribunal** | Eviction disputes under Karnataka Rent Act 1999 | Notified cities |

## 2. What to Bring

### Essential Documents (Originals + 2 Photocopies)
- **Identity proof**: Aadhaar, Voter ID, or Passport
- **Address proof**: Utility bill, ration card
- **Case-specific documents**:
  - Eviction: Rent agreement, rent receipts, notice received
  - DV: DIR Form I (if filed), medical reports, photos of injuries
  - Senior Citizen: Property documents, maintenance proof, relationship evidence
- **Vakalatnama** (if represented by advocate)
- **Court fee stamps** (purchase from court canteen or nearby stamp vendor)
- **Index of documents** filed with petition
- **Copy of your petition/application** for reference

### Personal Items
- Water bottle (plastic only, no glass)
- Notebook and pen
- Phone on silent (photography strictly prohibited in court premises)
- Cash for photocopies, stamps, auto/parking

## 3. Dress Code

| Acceptable | NOT Acceptable |
|-----------|---------------|
| Formal/semi-formal clothing | Shorts, sleeveless tops |
| Saree, salwar kameez | Slippers/flip-flops |
| Shirt and trousers | Torn/dirty clothing |
| Closed footwear | Hats/caps inside courtroom |

> **Tip**: Judges notice respectful appearance. Clean, modest clothing shows respect for the court.

## 4. Court Etiquette

### Before Entering
- Arrive 30 minutes early on first hearing
- Check the **cause list** (displayed on notice board or e-courts website) for your case number and court number
- Keep your phone on **silent mode**
- Switch off recording devices

### Inside the Courtroom
- Stand when the judge enters or exits
- Address the judge as **"Your Honour"** (Magistrate) or **"My Lord/My Lady"** (District/High Court)
- Speak only when spoken to, or when the judge calls your case
- Stand when speaking to the judge
- Do NOT argue with the opposing party directly — address the judge
- Do NOT interrupt when the judge or opposing counsel is speaking
- Bow slightly when entering/leaving the courtroom

### When Your Case Is Called
1. Stand up when the court reader calls your case number
2. Move to the front — stand at the designated place (usually a podium/railing)
3. If you have an advocate, let them speak. If appearing in person (*party-in-person*), address the judge directly
4. Answer questions clearly and briefly
5. If asked to submit documents, hand them to the court reader, NOT directly to the judge

## 5. Common Court Proceedings

### First Hearing (Filing Day)
- Court verifies petition completeness
- Issues **summons/notice** to the opposite party
- Sets next date (usually 15-30 days later)
- **You may not need to argue anything on the first day**

### Interim/Urgent Applications
- If you filed an interim application (e.g., stay on eviction, protection order):
  - Judge may hear it on the first day itself
  - Be prepared to explain urgency briefly (2-3 sentences)
  - Judge may pass an *ex parte* order (without hearing the other side) if urgency is clear

### Regular Hearing
- Both sides present arguments
- Documents are exhibited (formally admitted as evidence)
- Cross-examination of witnesses may happen
- Judge dictates the order

### Order/Judgment Day
- Judge reads the order in open court
- Obtain a **certified copy** from the copy section (costs ₹5-10 per page)
- Note the next date if any

## 6. Key Legal Terms You'll Hear

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Vakalatnama** | Power of attorney authorizing your lawyer to appear for you |
| **Petition/Plaint** | Your formal complaint document |
| **Respondent/Defendant** | The person you filed against |
| **Ex parte** | Order passed without hearing the other side (due to absence) |
| **Adjournment** | Postponement to a later date |
| **Interim order** | Temporary order until final judgment |
| **Stay order** | Court direction to stop something (e.g., eviction) |
| **Summons** | Court order requiring someone to appear |
| **Cause list** | Daily list of cases to be heard |
| **Next date** | The date your case will come up again |
| **Certified copy** | Official court-stamped copy of an order |

## 7. If Appearing Without a Lawyer (Party-in-Person)

### Your Rights
- You have the **right to represent yourself** in any court
- Courts are required to assist party-in-person litigants (CPC Order III, Rule 1)
- Judge may ask leading questions to help you present your case

### Tips
- Prepare a **written note** of key points (1 page max)
- Bring an extra copy for the judge
- Speak in Kannada or English (courts in Karnataka accept both)
- State facts, not opinions
- Say "I don't know" if unsure — don't guess
- Ask the judge if you can submit a written statement later if you feel nervous

---

## 8. After the Hearing

### Immediate Steps
1. Note the **next date** from the order sheet
2. Obtain **certified copy** of any order passed (apply at the copy section)
3. Keep all documents safe in a labeled folder
4. If the judge passed an order in your favour, confirm with the court reader what steps to follow for execution

### If the Order Is Against You
- You have the right to **appeal** (usually within 30-90 days depending on the court)
- Consult a lawyer before the appeal deadline
- File for a **stay** on the adverse order if needed

## 9. E-Courts Access (Check Case Status Online)

- **Website**: https://ecourts.gov.in/ecourts_home/
- **Mobile App**: eCourts Services (available on Android/iOS)
- Search by: Case number, party name, advocate name, FIR number
- Features: Case status, next hearing date, order copy download, cause list
