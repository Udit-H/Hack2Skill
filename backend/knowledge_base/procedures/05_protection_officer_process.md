# Protection Officer Process — DV Act 2005

> How to approach a Protection Officer, what they must do, and what happens if they refuse.

---

## 1. Who Is a Protection Officer?

- Appointed by the **State Government** under Section 8 of the PWDVA 2005
- Usually a government officer (Women & Child Development Department)
- Present in every district — typically at the District Women & Child Development Office
- Their role is to **assist the aggrieved person**, NOT to act as police

### Key Distinction
| Protection Officer | Police |
|---|---|
| Civil remedy — assists with court petitions | Criminal remedy — FIR, arrest |
| Cannot arrest anyone | Can arrest under BNS 85-86 (IPC 498A) |
| Helps file DIR and Section 12 petition | Registers FIR and investigates |
| Works under Magistrate's direction | Works under criminal law independently |

---

## 2. How to Find Your Protection Officer

### Step 1: District Office
- Visit the **District Women & Child Development Office**
- Every district in Karnataka has at least one Protection Officer
- Address available at: https://dwcd.karnataka.gov.in

### Step 2: One-Stop Centre (Sakhi)
- One-Stop Centres (set up under Sambal scheme) have a liaison with Protection Officers
- Available in every district — call **181** to locate yours

### Step 3: Court
- If you file a Section 12 petition directly, the **Magistrate will appoint** a Protection Officer for your case

### Step 4: Helplines
- **Women Helpline**: 181
- **NCW Helpline**: 7827-170-170
- **Police (non-emergency)**: 100

## 3. What the Protection Officer Must Do (Section 9)

### Mandatory Duties (Cannot Refuse)
1. **Inform** the aggrieved person of her rights under PWDVA
2. **Assist** in filing a **Domestic Incident Report** (DIR — Form I)
3. **Forward** the DIR to the Magistrate and the police
4. Help the person get **medical aid** from a medical facility
5. Help her find **safe shelter** if needed
6. Assist in filing the **Section 12 petition** before the Magistrate
7. Ensure the **respondent is served** with court orders
8. Help the person access **free legal aid** through DLSA/KSLSA

### On Court Order (Additional Duties)
- Serve protection orders to the respondent
- Assist in getting a **monetary relief order** enforced
- Monitor compliance with protection orders
- Report breaches to the Magistrate

## 4. Filing a Domestic Incident Report (DIR — Form I)

### What It Is
- Official record of domestic violence in a prescribed government form
- Different from an FIR (DIR is civil, FIR is criminal)
- Prepared BY the Protection Officer based on the victim's narration

### What It Contains
- Full details of the aggrieved person and respondent
- Relationship description
- Details of each type of violence (physical, sexual, emotional, economic)
- Details of children involved
- List of reliefs sought (protection, residence, monetary, custody)
- Medical report (if available)

### Process
1. Visit the Protection Officer
2. Narrate what happened — the PO fills in the form
3. **You do NOT need documents** to file a DIR (your verbal statement is enough)
4. PO signs and gives you a copy
5. PO forwards copies to: Magistrate + local police station (SHO)
6. Timeline: **Must be completed on the same day** the person visits

### Important
- A DIR can be filed even without filing a police FIR
- A DIR can be filed even years after the violence occurred
- A DIR is the foundation for filing a Section 12 petition
- Protection Officer CANNOT refuse to record a DIR

---

## 5. From DIR to Court (Section 12 Petition Flow)

```
Step 1: Visit Protection Officer
         ↓
Step 2: DIR (Form I) is prepared
         ↓
Step 3: PO forwards DIR to Magistrate + Police SHO
         ↓
Step 4: PO helps you file Section 12 Petition
        (Application for Protection/Residence/Monetary/Custody orders)
         ↓
Step 5: Magistrate issues Notice to Respondent
         ↓
Step 6: First Hearing (within 3 days of filing — S.12(4))
         ↓
Step 7: Magistrate may pass Interim Protection Order (ex parte)
         ↓
Step 8: Regular hearings — both sides heard
         ↓
Step 9: Final Order (must be within 60 days — S.12(5))
```

## 6. If the Protection Officer Refuses

### Common Excuses (All Illegal)
- "We don't handle DV cases here"
- "Go to the police first"
- "You need documents/evidence first"
- "Come back tomorrow/next week"
- "This is a family matter, sort it out yourselves"

### Remedies

#### Option A: Complain to District Magistrate
- Protection Officers work under the DM's supervision
- Write a complaint with the PO's name, date, and what they refused
- DM can direct the PO to act immediately

#### Option B: File Petition Directly with Magistrate
- You do NOT need a DIR to file a Section 12 petition
- You can approach the Magistrate directly (Section 12(1))
- The Magistrate will then appoint/direct a PO

#### Option C: Approach Service Provider (NGO)
- Registered NGOs under Section 10 of PWDVA can:
  - Record an incident report (similar to DIR)
  - Forward it to the Magistrate directly
  - Assist with filing Section 12 petitions
- Contact any registered women's organisation or shelter home

#### Option D: File RTI
- Protection Officer records are public information
- File RTI asking for: number of DIRs filed, appointments register, case disposal records
- This creates pressure for compliance

#### Option E: SHRC / NCW Complaint
- State Human Rights Commission: File complaint for dereliction of duty
- National Commission for Women: Complaint portal at ncw.nic.in

## 8. Service Providers (NGOs) as Alternative to Protection Officer

Under Section 10 of PWDVA, registered service providers can:
- Record domestic incidents
- Get the aggrieved person medical care
- Provide shelter
- Forward information to the Protection Officer/Magistrate

### Registered Organisations in Karnataka
- **Vimochana** — Bengaluru (080-25491782)
- **Stree Jagriti Samiti** — Bengaluru
- **Women's Initiative (WINS)** — Bengaluru
- **Mahila Sahayavani** — State-wide (181)
