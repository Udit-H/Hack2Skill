# Senior Citizen Tribunal Process — Karnataka

> Complete guide to filing complaints under the Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007.

---

## 1. Who Can File?

### Eligible Persons
- **Parents** (including adoptive parents) aged 60+ who are unable to maintain themselves
- **Senior citizens** (60+) who have transferred property to children/relatives and are being neglected
- **Grandparents** can file against grandchildren if grandchildren inherited property meant for the grandparent's maintenance

### Who You File Against
- **Children** (sons, daughters, including married daughters)
- **Legal heirs** who have inherited or will inherit property
- **Relatives** (NOT being children) who possess the senior citizen's property or are in line to inherit it
- "Children" includes **son-in-law and daughter-in-law** per Karnataka amendment

### No Filing Fee
- Applications under this Act are **completely free** — no court fee stamps needed

---

## 2. Where to File

### Primary: Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM)
- The Tribunal under this Act is the **SDM office** of the area where the senior citizen resides
- OR where the respondent (child/relative) resides
- Each district has multiple SDM offices covering different taluks

### How to Find Your SDM Office
- Visit the District Collector's website for your district
- Or call **1800-180-1551** (Elder Helpline)
- Karnataka SDM offices are at every taluk headquarters

### Alternative Filing Points
- **District Magistrate (DM/DC)** — can hear appeals and also accept original complaints
- **Appellate Tribunal** — for appeals against SDM orders (District Collector or designee)

---

## 3. Types of Relief Available

### A. Maintenance (Section 5)
- Monthly maintenance amount from children/relatives
- **Maximum**: ₹10,000/month per applicant (per child)
- Covers: food, clothing, shelter, medical care
- Can be awarded to BOTH parents jointly or individually
- **Interim maintenance** can be ordered within 30 days of notice

### B. Property Restoration (Section 23)
- If a senior citizen transferred property to a child/relative under the condition of maintenance:
  - AND that child/relative is not providing maintenance or basic amenities
  - The transfer is **deemed VOID** — can be cancelled by the Tribunal
- Applies to: gifts, sale deeds, settlement deeds, power of attorney transfers
- Property reverts back to the senior citizen

### C. Protection from Eviction
- If a senior citizen is living in property owned by them but children are trying to evict:
  - Tribunal can order that they NOT be evicted
  - Applies even if property was transferred, if conditions of maintenance were attached

### D. Protection from Abuse
- If children/relatives are causing physical/emotional abuse:
  - Tribunal can order them to stop
  - Can order removal of the abusive person from the shared household
  - Penalty: Up to ₹5,000 fine or up to 3 months imprisonment

## 4. Step-by-Step Filing Process

### Step 1: Write the Application
- Plain paper application (no stamp paper needed)
- Can be handwritten or typed
- In Kannada or English

**Include:**
- Your name, age, address
- Names and addresses of children/relatives you're filing against
- Relationship (son, daughter, grandson, etc.)
- Details of what you need (maintenance amount, property restoration, etc.)
- Facts: What happened, when did they stop maintaining you
- Attach: Aadhaar card, any property documents (if claiming property restoration)

### Step 2: Submit to SDM Office
- Go to the SDM office in person
- OR have someone submit on your behalf (no vakalatnama needed)
- The Tribunal can also take **suo motu** cognizance if informed by any person
- You can also send the application by **registered post**

### Step 3: Notice to Respondent
- SDM office issues notice to the children/relatives
- Notice period: **minimum 15 days** to respond
- Service: By post, personal service, or through police

### Step 4: Hearing
- Both parties appear before the SDM
- Informal hearing — no strict courtroom procedures
- Senior citizen can bring **any person** to assist them (does not have to be a lawyer)
- SDM may refer the matter to a **Conciliation Officer** first

### Step 5: Conciliation (Optional)
- Conciliation Officer (appointed by Tribunal) tries to mediate
- If settlement is reached → recorded by Tribunal → binding
- If no settlement → matter goes back to Tribunal for adjudication
- Conciliation must be completed within **1 month**

### Step 6: Tribunal Order
- SDM passes order after hearing both sides
- **Must be completed within 90 days** of application filing (S.7(4))
- Order can include:
  - Monthly maintenance amount (up to ₹10,000/month per parent)
  - Interim maintenance (within 30 days of first hearing)
  - Property restoration
  - Protection orders

### Step 7: Enforcement
- If the respondent doesn't comply with the maintenance order:
  - Application for execution to the same SDM
  - SDM can impose **fine up to ₹5,000** or **imprisonment up to 1 month** for each default
  - Arrears of maintenance are recoverable as "arrears of land revenue" (S.5(8))
  - This means the District Collector can attach and sell property to recover maintenance

---

## 5. Appeal Process

| Stage | Authority | Time Limit |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Original order | SDM (Tribunal) | Within 90 days |
| Appeal | Appellate Tribunal (District Collector or designee) | Within **30 days** of SDM order |
| Revision | High Court of Karnataka | Under Section 482 CrPC |

- **No second appeal** — only revision petition to High Court
- Appellate Tribunal must decide within **1 month** of receiving appeal (S.16(4))

---

## 6. Interim Relief (Emergency)

If the senior citizen needs **immediate maintenance** before full hearing:

- File application for **interim maintenance** along with main application
- SDM **must** pass interim order within **30 days** of notice to respondent
- Interim maintenance continues until final order is passed
- Respondent must pay from the **date of application** (not from date of order)

---

## 7. Special Provisions — Karnataka

### Karnataka Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Rules, 2009

- **Maintenance Limit**: Up to ₹10,000/month (national limit, applicable in Karnataka)
- **Tribunal**: SDM of each taluk acts as the Tribunal
- **Appellate Authority**: District Commissioner (DC)
- **Conciliation Officer**: Appointed from among social workers, retired officers
- **Helpline**: 1800-180-1551 (Karnataka Elder Helpline)

### Karnataka State Policy for Senior Citizens
- Free bus travel (KSRTC) for citizens 65+
- Priority at government hospitals
- Reservation in housing schemes
- Old Age Pension: ₹600/month (BPL) under Sandhya Suraksha Yojana

## 8. Documents Helpful (But Not Mandatory)

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Aadhaar card | Identity proof |
| Age proof (birth certificate, school record) | Confirm senior citizen status (60+) |
| Property documents (sale deed, gift deed) | If claiming property restoration |
| Bank statements | Show financial need |
| Medical records | Show health/maintenance needs |
| Photos of living conditions | Evidence of neglect |
| Witness statements | Corroborate neglect claims |

> **Important**: The Tribunal follows a **summary procedure** — strict rules of evidence do NOT apply. The senior citizen's oral statement alone is sufficient to initiate proceedings.

---

## 9. Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Offence | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| Abandonment of senior citizen (S.24) | Fine up to ₹5,000 OR imprisonment up to 3 months OR both |
| Non-payment of maintenance ordered by Tribunal | Warrant + recovery as arrears of land revenue |
| Breach of protection order | Prosecution under S.24 |

- These are **cognizable and non-bailable** offences
- Any police officer can arrest without warrant

---

## 10. Practical Tips

1. **Start with conciliation**: Many family disputes resolve with mediation. The Tribunal can refer to a Conciliation Officer — this is faster than full proceedings
2. **File interim application**: Don't wait for the final order if you need money urgently
3. **Multiple children**: You can file against ALL children in one application — the Tribunal will divide the maintenance among them based on their ability
4. **Even if you have pension/income**: You can still claim maintenance if your income is insufficient for your medical and living expenses
5. **Old Age Homes**: If your children refuse to maintain you, the State Government must provide old age home facilities under Section 19
6. **Free legal aid**: If you can't afford a lawyer, contact KSLSA (Karnataka State Legal Services Authority) or your district DLSA
