## One-Stop Centres (Sakhi Centres) in Karnataka

One-Stop Centres provide integrated services for women affected by violence:
- Police assistance
- Legal aid
- Medical examination
- Counselling (psycho-social)
- Temporary shelter (up to 5 days)

### Locations in Bangalore
- **Bangalore Urban**: One-Stop Centre, attached to District Women and Child Development Office.
- Contact: Call 181 (Women Helpline) for nearest Sakhi Centre address and directions.

### Services Available
1. **Emergency Response**: 24/7 help through 181 helpline.
2. **Police Facilitation**: Police officer at centre to help file FIR/DIR.
3. **Medical Aid**: Doctor/nurse for immediate medical examination and MLC.
4. **Legal Counselling**: Lawyer for free legal advice and case filing.
5. **Psycho-Social Counselling**: Professional counsellor for emotional support.
6. **Temporary Shelter**: Up to 5 days of safe shelter.
7. **Referral**: To long-term shelter homes, legal aid clinics, rehabilitation.

## Shelter Homes for Domestic Violence Victims in Karnataka

### Government Shelter Homes
- **Swadhar Greh**: Government-funded shelter homes for women in difficult circumstances.
  - Provides: shelter, food, clothing, medical care, counselling, vocational training.
  - Duration: Up to 3 years (extendable).
  - Eligibility: Women and girls who are DV victims, homeless, abandoned, rescued from trafficking.
  - No charges.

### How to Access Shelter
1. **Call 181** (Women Helpline) — they coordinate shelter placement.
2. **Approach Protection Officer** at District Women and Child Development Office.
3. **File DIR** — Protection Officer is duty-bound to arrange shelter under PWDVA Section 6.
4. **Through NGOs**: Several NGOs run shelter homes and can arrange immediate placement.

### Key NGOs Operating Shelter Homes in Bangalore
- **Vimochana** (women's rights organization)
- **Parihar** (crisis intervention centre by Bangalore Police)
- **Stree Jagruti Samiti** (women's awareness organization)
- **BOSCO** (for children and women)
- Contact through 181 helpline or Protection Officer for current addresses and availability.

## Legal Aid Clinics in Bangalore

### DLSA (District Legal Services Authority) — Bangalore Urban
- **Location**: City Civil Court Complex, Nrupathunga Road, Bangalore.
- **Hours**: Monday to Saturday, 10:30 AM to 5:00 PM.
- **Services**: Free legal advice, lawyer assignment, legal aid applications, Lok Adalat.

### Taluk Legal Services Committee
- Located at taluk court complexes.
- Same services as DLSA at local level.

### KSLSA (Karnataka State Legal Services Authority)
- **Address**: Nyaya Seva Soudha, near High Court, Bangalore.
- **Phone**: 080-22111714
- **Services**: Coordination, monitoring, policy, and direct legal aid.

### Front Office Legal Aid Clinics
- At all district court complexes.
- Walk-in facility. No appointment needed.
- Lawyer available for immediate consultation.

## Karnataka State Women's Commission

### What They Handle
- Complaints of domestic violence.
- Sexual harassment at workplace.
- Denial of property rights.
- Dowry harassment.
- Any violation of women's rights.

### How to Complain
1. Written complaint to KSWC (in person, by post, or online).
2. KSWC issues notice to respondent.
3. Hearing conducted.
4. KSWC can: recommend action to police, order compensation, refer to court.
5. KSWC orders are recommendations (not directly enforceable as court orders) but carry significant weight.

### Advantage Over Court
- Faster process (weeks vs months in court).
- Less formal.
- Counselling and mediation available.
- Can handle cases that don't fit neatly into legal categories.

## Karnataka Social Welfare — Senior Citizens Services

### Sandhya Suraksha Yojana (Old Age Pension)
- Monthly pension for senior citizens (60+) who are destitute/below poverty line.
- Amount: ₹600-2,000 per month (varies by category and age).
- Apply at: Taluk Social Welfare Office with age proof and income certificate.

### Vayo Vruddhara Yojana
- Health insurance and medical support for senior citizens.
- Free health checkups, medicines, and hospital treatment.

### District Social Welfare Officer (DSWO)
- Contact DSWO at district office for all senior citizen welfare schemes.
- They can help with: pension applications, old age home referral, medical support, legal referral to Senior Citizens Tribunal.

## When to Use Which Helpline — Quick Reference

### Immediate Danger / Physical Violence RIGHT NOW
→ Call **112** (Universal Emergency) or **100** (Police)

### DV — Need Help but Not Immediate Emergency
→ Call **181** (Women Helpline) — they coordinate police, shelter, legal aid

### Need Legal Advice
→ Call **15100** (NALSA Legal Aid) or **1516** (Tele-Law)

### Senior Citizen in Distress
→ Call **14567** (Elder Helpline)

### Electricity/Water Illegally Cut by Landlord
→ Call **1912** (BESCOM) or **1916** (BWSSB), then file FIR
